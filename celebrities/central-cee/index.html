Where does Central Cee eat in London?

Central Cee is rooted in Shepherd's Bush, West London, one of the city's most diverse food neighborhoods. Frequently Asked Questions

Where does Central Cee eat in London?
Central Cee frequents West London spots near his Shepherd's Bush roots, including multicultural street food markets and local chicken shops. As his career has grown, he's also been spotted at Mayfair restaurants like Nobu and Sexy Fish, and Soho dining destinations.
What is Central Cee's ethnic background?
Central Cee (born Oakley Neil H.T. Caesar-Su) has Irish, Guyanese, and Chinese heritage. This multicultural background is reflected in his diverse food tastes, spanning Caribbean, Chinese, and British cuisines - all easily found in his West London neighborhood.
Does Central Cee eat street food?
Yes, Central Cee grew up around Shepherd's Bush Market, one of London's most diverse food markets. Street food from Caribbean, Middle Eastern, and Chinese vendors has been part of his life since childhood. The chicken shop culture is also central to UK drill identity.
Where does Central Cee eat in NYC?
When in New York - often linked to his relationship with Ice Spice - Central Cee has been spotted at celebrity hotspots like TAO Downtown and upscale dining establishments in Manhattan. He bridges London and NYC food cultures seamlessly.
What is Central Cee's favorite food?
While specific favorites aren't extensively documented, Central Cee's multicultural heritage points to Caribbean food (jerk chicken, rice and peas), Chinese cuisine (dim sum, roast duck), and London staples (chicken shop). His food world spans from Shepherd's Bush street food to Mayfair fine dining.
