What is George W. Bush's favorite food?

George W. Bush is quintessentially Texan when it comes to food. He loves Texas BBQ, particularly brisket, along with Tex-Mex cuisine. He's also known for enjoying cheeseburgers, peanut butter and jelly sandwiches, and hosting casual BBQ gatherings at his Crawford ranch for world leaders.

Crawford Ranch BBQ Diplomacy

Bush's Prairie Chapel Ranch in Crawford, Texas became famous for casual "BBQ diplomacy" with world leaders:

  • Putin Visit 2001: Russian leader hosted for Texas BBQ
  • World Leaders: Multiple heads of state enjoyed casual ranch hospitality
  • Ranch Style: Casual dress, outdoor grilling, Texas hospitality
  • The Menu: BBQ, Tex-Mex, comfort food, and pecan pie

The Famous Pretzel Incident

January 13, 2002

One of the most memorable food moments in presidential history:

  • Bush was watching a football game at the White House
  • He choked on a pretzel and briefly lost consciousness
  • Fell off the couch and had a bruise on his face
  • Made light of it, joking "my mother was right - always chew your pretzels before you swallow"

Frequently Asked Questions

What is George W. Bush's favorite food?
Bush is known for loving Texas BBQ, especially brisket, along with Tex-Mex cuisine. He also enjoys cheeseburgers and famously loves peanut butter and jelly sandwiches.
What happened with George W. Bush and the pretzel?
In January 2002, Bush choked on a pretzel while watching a football game at the White House and briefly fainted. He had a bruise on his face but recovered quickly and made jokes about it, saying his mother always told him to chew his pretzels.
Does George W. Bush cook at his ranch?
Yes, Bush is known for hosting BBQs at his Crawford, Texas ranch. He practiced "BBQ diplomacy" by inviting world leaders for casual Texas-style gatherings with grilled food and Tex-Mex.
Where does George W. Bush eat BBQ in Texas?
While specific restaurants aren't always confirmed, Bush enjoys the Dallas and Austin BBQ scenes. Texas BBQ legends like Pecan Lodge in Dallas and Franklin Barbecue in Austin represent the style he loves.
What did George W. Bush serve at his ranch?
Bush served Texas-style BBQ, Tex-Mex, comfort food, and pecan pie to guests at his Crawford ranch. The casual dress and outdoor cooking became known as "BBQ diplomacy" when hosting foreign leaders.
