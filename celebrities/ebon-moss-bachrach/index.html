What is the famous "forks" episode from The Bear?

In Season 2, Episode 7 ("Forks"), Richie stages at Ever, a fine dining restaurant in Chicago. Ebon Moss-Bachrach trained at real Chicago fine dining establishments to authentically portray this transformative experience where Richie learns about hospitality, service, and finding purpose through food.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the "forks" speech from The Bear?
In Season 2, Episode 7, Richie stages at the fine dining restaurant Ever and learns about hospitality. The emotional episode culminates in Richie's transformation as he learns the meaning of service, represented by the meticulous polishing of forks.
Did Ebon Moss-Bachrach train at real restaurants for The Bear?
Yes! Ebon researched at Chicago's top restaurants including Mr. Beef for Italian beef authenticity and fine dining establishments like Ever for the famous "Forks" episode. He learned real service and hospitality skills.
What restaurant inspired the "Forks" episode?
Ever, the two-Michelin-star Chicago restaurant by Curtis Duffy, was a primary inspiration for the fictional fine dining restaurant in the episode. The show's team consulted with Ever's staff.
Where is Ebon Moss-Bachrach from?
Ebon Moss-Bachrach was born in Amherst, Massachusetts in 1977. He grew up in New England before moving to New York City to pursue acting, where he built his career in theater and television.
What other shows has Ebon Moss-Bachrach been in?
Before The Bear, Ebon was known for playing Desi on Girls (HBO) and Micro on The Punisher (Netflix). His theater background in NYC also shaped his appreciation for post-show dining culture.
