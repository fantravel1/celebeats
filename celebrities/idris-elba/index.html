Idris Elba

British acting icon and international DJ. From East London roots to global stages, his culinary journey spans Caribbean heritage, London fine dining, and Ibiza nights.

Where does Idris Elba like to eat?

Idris Elba embraces London's diverse food scene, from East End Caribbean spots reflecting his Sierra Leonean and Ghanaian heritage to upscale Mayfair restaurants. As DJ Big Driis, he's also familiar with club scenes and late-night dining from Ibiza to Los Angeles.

Tier B EvidenceMedium Confidence

London Favorites

East London Roots

Born and raised in Hackney, Idris has deep connections to East London's vibrant food scene:

  • Caribbean restaurants in Hackney - Jerk chicken, rice and peas
  • Brixton Market - African and Caribbean food stalls
  • East London street food - Multicultural flavors

His father was from Sierra Leone and his mother from Ghana, giving him an appreciation for West African cuisine.

Upscale London

  • Chiltern Firehouse - Marylebone celebrity hotspot
  • Nobu London - Japanese fine dining
  • Sexy Fish - Mayfair Asian restaurant
  • Scott's - Mayfair seafood institution
  • The Ivy - Classic British dining

The DJ Lifestyle

DJ Big Driis

Idris Elba has been DJing since the 1990s and performs internationally under the name DJ Big Driis. His DJ career has taken him to:

  • Coachella - California music festival
  • Glastonbury - UK's biggest festival
  • Ibiza - Hi Ibiza, Pacha, Amnesia
  • Global nightclubs - From Miami to Tokyo

The DJ lifestyle means late nights, post-show dining, and experiencing food scenes across the globe. Ibiza's restaurant scene, from beach clubs to Michelin-starred dining, is familiar territory.

African & Caribbean Heritage

With a Sierra Leonean father and Ghanaian mother, Idris grew up with West African and Caribbean flavors:

  • Jollof Rice - West African staple (he's joked about the Ghana vs Nigeria debate)
  • Jerk Chicken - Caribbean classic
  • Fried Plantain - Sweet, caramelized perfection
  • Egusi Soup - Nigerian/Ghanaian specialty
  • Ackee and Saltfish - Jamaican breakfast

He's spoken about how his multicultural upbringing in London exposed him to diverse cuisines from an early age.

Global Dining

Los Angeles

When filming in LA, Idris has been spotted at:

  • Craig's - West Hollywood celebrity spot
  • Catch LA - Rooftop scene dining
  • Soho House - Members-only club

Ibiza

His DJ residencies bring him to the White Isle regularly:

  • Nobu Ibiza - Beach club meets Japanese cuisine
  • Lio Ibiza - Dinner and show
  • La Paloma - Italian in San Lorenzo
