What does Peso Pluma like to eat?

Peso Pluma (born Hassan Emilio Kabande Laija) grew up in Zapopan, Jalisco - the heartland of Mexican cuisine. His Jalisco roots connect him to birria, tortas ahogadas, tequila, and the rich culinary traditions of western Mexico. Now a global superstar, he dines at top spots in Mexico City, Los Angeles, and Las Vegas.

Jalisco Food Heritage

From the Heart of Mexican Cuisine

Jalisco is one of Mexico's most important culinary regions. Growing up in Zapopan, Peso Pluma was immersed in iconic dishes:

  • Birria - Jalisco's signature slow-cooked meat stew, now viral worldwide
  • Tortas Ahogadas - "Drowned" sandwiches in spicy chile de arbol sauce, a Guadalajara staple
  • Tequila - Born in the land of tequila, Jalisco is the only place authentic tequila is produced
  • Pozole - Traditional hominy stew served for celebrations
  • Jericalla - Jalisco's signature custard dessert

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Peso Pluma's favorite food?
Peso Pluma is from Zapopan, Jalisco, one of Mexico's great culinary regions. His food roots include birria (Jalisco's signature stew), tortas ahogadas (drowned sandwiches), pozole, and the incredible street food of western Mexico.
Where does Peso Pluma eat in Mexico City?
Peso Pluma has been spotted at Mexico City's top restaurants including Pujol (world-famous fine dining), Contramar (iconic seafood), and classic taco spots. He frequents establishments in Polanco, Roma Norte, and Condesa.
Does Peso Pluma have a tequila brand?
As a native of Jalisco - the only region where authentic tequila can be produced - Peso Pluma has deep connections to tequila culture. He's been associated with premium tequila brands and frequently references tequila in his music and lifestyle.
What is Peso Pluma's real name?
Peso Pluma's real name is Hassan Emilio Kabande Laija. He was born on June 15, 1999, in Zapopan, Jalisco, Mexico. His stage name "Peso Pluma" translates to "featherweight" in English.
Where does Peso Pluma eat in Los Angeles?
In LA, Peso Pluma gravitates toward authentic Mexican restaurants including spots in East LA, creative Mexican-American eateries in the Arts District, and Jalisco-rooted food trucks like Mariscos Jalisco that remind him of home.
