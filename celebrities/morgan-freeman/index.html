Morgan Freeman

Oscar-winning actor and blues club owner with deep Mississippi Delta roots. Co-owner of Ground Zero Blues Club in Clarksdale, celebrating Southern food and music.

Does Morgan Freeman own a restaurant?

Yes! Morgan Freeman co-owns Ground Zero Blues Club in Clarksdale, Mississippi with businessman Bill Luckett. The juke joint-style establishment serves Southern comfort food alongside live blues music, celebrating the Delta's rich musical heritage where the blues was born.

Ground Zero Blues Club

The Delta's Premier Juke Joint

Located in Clarksdale, Mississippi - the heart of the Delta blues - Ground Zero Blues Club opened in 2001. Freeman and Luckett created an authentic juke joint experience with:

  • Live blues music - Every weekend and many weeknights
  • Southern comfort food - Classic Delta cuisine
  • Juke joint atmosphere - Graffiti-covered walls, raw energy
  • Celebrity sightings - Freeman visits regularly when in town

Ground Zero Menu Highlights

  • Delta Tamales - Mississippi tradition
  • Fried Catfish - Southern staple
  • BBQ Pulled Pork - Slow-smoked perfection
  • Fried Pickles - Classic appetizer
  • Pecan Pie - Southern dessert icon

Mississippi Delta Connection

Morgan Freeman has called Mississippi home for decades, owning a 126-acre ranch in Charleston. His connection to the Delta runs deep, and he's become an ambassador for the region's culture and cuisine.

The Mississippi Delta is the birthplace of the blues, and Freeman has worked to preserve and celebrate this heritage through his investments in the region, including Ground Zero and other local businesses.

"The Delta is where I feel most at home. The food, the music, the people - it's all connected."

Southern Food Favorites

Delta Comfort Food

Morgan Freeman's food preferences reflect his deep Southern roots:

  • Fried Catfish - The Delta's signature dish
  • Fried Chicken - Sunday dinner staple
  • Delta Tamales - A Mississippi tradition different from Mexican tamales
  • Collard Greens - Slow-cooked with ham hocks
  • Cornbread - Essential Southern bread
  • Sweet Tea - The house wine of the South

Beekeeping & Honey

In recent years, Morgan Freeman has become a passionate beekeeper, converting his 126-acre Mississippi ranch into a bee sanctuary. He keeps 26 hives and has become an advocate for bee conservation.

The honey from his hives represents another connection to the land and to natural, wholesome food. Freeman doesn't wear protective gear when tending his bees - he says he's never been stung.
