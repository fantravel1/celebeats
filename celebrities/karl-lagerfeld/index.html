What were Karl Lagerfeld's dining preferences?

Karl Lagerfeld was famously disciplined about his diet. After his dramatic weight loss in 2001, he maintained a strict regimen of steamed vegetables, grilled fish, and Diet Coke. In Paris, he was a longtime regular at Cafe de Flore and appreciated Japanese cuisine for its lightness and presentation.

The Lagerfeld Philosophy

Lifestyle

Karl Lagerfeld approached food with the same discipline and aesthetic sense he brought to fashion:

  • "I am like a caricature of myself" - His diet was part of his carefully curated image
  • Discipline Over Indulgence - Prioritized maintaining his silhouette over culinary pleasures
  • Presentation Matters - Appreciated Japanese cuisine for its visual artistry
  • Social Dining - Despite diet restrictions, maintained his presence at Paris's elite establishments

His approach to food reflected his larger philosophy: every aspect of life should be controlled and aesthetically considered.

Frequently Asked Questions

Karl Lagerfeld frequented refined Parisian establishments including Cafe de Flore, where he was a regular for decades. He also enjoyed Le Voltaire and various upscale restaurants in the 7th arrondissement near the Chanel headquarters. His presence at Cafe de Flore was so iconic that he had a regular table.

Karl Lagerfeld famously followed a strict diet to maintain his figure. He lost over 90 pounds in 2001 and maintained it by eating primarily steamed vegetables, grilled fish, and drinking Diet Coke. He detailed his approach in "The Karl Lagerfeld Diet" book, co-written with his nutritionist.

While Karl lived in Paris for decades, he was known for preferring lighter fare to traditional heavy French cuisine. He appreciated Japanese food, steamed vegetables, and simple grilled dishes rather than rich French preparations. His diet was deliberately contrary to indulgent French dining traditions.

Karl Lagerfeld was famous for his Diet Coke consumption, reportedly drinking up to 10 cans daily. It became part of his iconic image alongside his fingerless gloves and white ponytail. He preferred it as a zero-calorie beverage that fit his strict dietary regimen.
