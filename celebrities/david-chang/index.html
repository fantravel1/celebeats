What restaurants does David Chang own?

David Chang owns the Momofuku restaurant group, which includes Momofuku Noodle Bar (his original NYC spot), Momofuku Ko (2 Michelin stars), Fuku (fried chicken sandwiches), Majordomo (LA), and numerous other locations across NYC, LA, Las Vegas, and Toronto. He revolutionized Korean-American cuisine and ramen in America.

The Momofuku Empire

From One Noodle Bar to Global Phenomenon

David Chang opened the original Momofuku Noodle Bar in 2004 in NYC's East Village with just 27 seats. The name "Momofuku" pays tribute to Momofuku Ando, inventor of instant ramen. What started as a small noodle shop has grown into:

  • 20+ restaurants worldwide
  • Momofuku Goods - consumer products line
  • Ugly Delicious - Netflix documentary series
  • The Dave Chang Show - popular podcast
  • Majordomo Media - production company

The Anti-Fine-Dining Philosophy

Revolutionizing Restaurant Culture

David Chang challenged fine dining conventions by:

  • No reservations policy (originally) - first come, first served
  • Loud music and casual atmosphere at serious restaurants
  • No tablecloths, no dress code, no pretension
  • Celebrating Asian flavors without "authenticity" gatekeeping
  • Open about mental health struggles in the restaurant industry

"I want to serve food that I want to eat. Everything else is secondary."

Frequently Asked Questions

What restaurants does David Chang own?
David Chang owns the Momofuku restaurant group including Momofuku Noodle Bar, Momofuku Ko (2 Michelin stars), Fuku, Ssam Bar, Majordomo in LA, and locations in Las Vegas and Toronto. He's grown from one 27-seat noodle bar to over 20 restaurants worldwide.
What is David Chang's most famous dish?
David Chang's most famous dish is the pork belly buns (gua bao) from Momofuku Noodle Bar - steamed buns filled with braised pork belly, hoisin, scallions, and cucumbers. His ramen and Bo Ssam (large-format pork shoulder) are also iconic dishes that defined the Momofuku experience.
What is Ugly Delicious about?
Ugly Delicious is David Chang's Netflix documentary series that explores food culture around the world. Each episode focuses on a different food (pizza, tacos, fried rice, BBQ) and examines cultural identity, authenticity debates, and the stories behind beloved dishes across different cultures.
Does Momofuku Ko have a Michelin star?
Yes, Momofuku Ko currently holds 2 Michelin stars. The intimate 12-seat counter restaurant offers a multi-course tasting menu experience. It's one of the most acclaimed restaurants in David Chang's empire and has maintained Michelin recognition since 2009.
What does Momofuku mean?
Momofuku is named after Momofuku Ando, the Taiwanese-Japanese inventor of instant ramen. The name translates roughly to "lucky peach" in Japanese, which also became the name of Chang's now-defunct food magazine. It's an homage to the democratization of noodles.
