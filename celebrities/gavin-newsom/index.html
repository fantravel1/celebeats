Does Gavin Newsom own restaurants?

Yes, Gavin Newsom co-founded the PlumpJack Group in 1992 with investor Gordon Getty. The company has grown to include wineries, restaurants, wine shops, hotels, and bars across California. This makes Newsom one of the only politicians who is literally a hospitality industry entrepreneur.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Gavin Newsom own restaurants?
Yes, Newsom co-founded the PlumpJack Group in 1992 with Gordon Getty. The company owns wineries, restaurants including Balboa Cafe, wine shops, and hotels across California.
What is PlumpJack?
PlumpJack is a hospitality company co-founded by Gavin Newsom. It includes PlumpJack Winery in Napa Valley, CADE Estate, Odette Estate, wine shops, Balboa Cafe, and the PlumpJack Squaw Valley Inn.
Where is PlumpJack Winery?
PlumpJack Winery is located in Oakville in Napa Valley, California. The PlumpJack Group also owns CADE Estate on Howell Mountain and Odette Estate in Stags Leap District.
Does Gavin Newsom drink wine?
Yes, Newsom is a wine enthusiast who co-founded multiple wineries in Napa Valley. PlumpJack Winery produces highly-rated Cabernet Sauvignon and other varietals.
What restaurants does Gavin Newsom own in San Francisco?
Through the PlumpJack Group, Newsom has ownership interests in Balboa Cafe, MatrixFillmore, and PlumpJack Wine & Spirits shops in San Francisco.
