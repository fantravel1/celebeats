What is Michelle Obama's White House Kitchen Garden?

Michelle Obama planted the White House Kitchen Garden in 2009, the first vegetable garden at the White House since Eleanor Roosevelt's Victory Garden. The 1,100 square foot garden grew over 55 varieties of vegetables and became a powerful symbol of her healthy eating advocacy, inspiring school gardens and nutrition education nationwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Michelle Obama's White House Kitchen Garden?
Michelle Obama planted the White House Kitchen Garden in 2009, the first vegetable garden since Eleanor Roosevelt's Victory Garden. The 1,100 square foot garden grew over 55 varieties of vegetables and became a symbol of her healthy eating advocacy.
What was Let's Move?
Let's Move! was Michelle Obama's initiative launched in 2010 to address childhood obesity. It promoted healthy eating, physical activity, and access to nutritious food, reaching millions of American children through schools and communities.
Where do the Obamas go on date nights?
The Obamas are known for romantic date nights at restaurants in NYC (including Emilio's Ballato in SoHo), DC, Martha's Vineyard, and Hawaii. They're often photographed looking happy at various restaurants.
What are Michelle Obama's favorite Chicago foods?
Michelle has Chicago South Side roots and appreciates Chicago classics including deep dish pizza, Chicago-style hot dogs (no ketchup!), and soul food from neighborhood institutions.
Does Michelle Obama cook?
Yes, Michelle has spoken about cooking for her family and the importance of family meals. She balanced cooking for Malia and Sasha with demanding schedules and wrote about food and family in her books.
