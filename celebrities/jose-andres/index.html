What is World Central Kitchen and what does Jose Andres do?

World Central Kitchen (WCK) is a non-profit organization founded by Chef Jose Andres in 2010 that provides meals to communities affected by disasters and humanitarian crises. From Hurricane Maria in Puerto Rico to the war in Ukraine, WCK has served hundreds of millions of meals worldwide. Andres is also a renowned restaurateur with over 30 establishments.

World Central Kitchen Humanitarian Work

Feeding the World in Crisis

Jose Andres founded World Central Kitchen in 2010, but it gained international recognition during Hurricane Maria in Puerto Rico (2017), where they served over 3.7 million meals. Since then, WCK has responded to countless emergencies:

  • Hurricane Maria (2017) - 3.7+ million meals in Puerto Rico
  • COVID-19 Pandemic (2020) - Tens of millions of meals across the US
  • Ukraine War (2022-present) - Over 200 million meals and counting
  • Gaza Crisis (2023-present) - Emergency food relief operations
  • Maui Wildfires (2023) - Immediate response and recovery meals
  • 2x Nobel Peace Prize nominee for humanitarian work

The Tapas Revolution in America

Changing How America Eats

When Jose Andres opened Jaleo in 1993, Americans didn't know what tapas were. He changed that:

  • Introduced small-plates dining to the American mainstream
  • Brought Ferran Adria's molecular gastronomy techniques to the US
  • Fought to legalize Iberico ham imports (succeeded in 2007)
  • Taught Americans about regional Spanish cuisine beyond paella
  • Became a naturalized US citizen in 2013

"A country's food tells you everything about its history, its culture, and its people."

Frequently Asked Questions

What is World Central Kitchen?
World Central Kitchen (WCK) is a non-profit organization founded by Chef Jose Andres in 2010. It provides meals to communities affected by natural disasters and humanitarian crises around the world. WCK has served hundreds of millions of meals in response to events like Hurricane Maria, the COVID-19 pandemic, the Ukraine war, and countless other emergencies.
What restaurants does Jose Andres own?
Jose Andres owns ThinkFoodGroup, which operates 30+ restaurants including Jaleo (Spanish tapas), Minibar (2 Michelin stars), The Bazaar, Zaytinya (Mediterranean), China Chilcano (Peruvian-Chinese), Oyamel (Mexican), and Somni (2 Michelin stars). His restaurants span Washington D.C., New York, Los Angeles, Miami, Las Vegas, and Disney Springs.
Has Jose Andres won a Nobel Peace Prize?
Jose Andres has been nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize twice but has not won. His nominations recognized World Central Kitchen's extraordinary humanitarian work feeding millions of people affected by disasters and conflicts worldwide. He continues to be one of the most impactful humanitarian figures in the culinary world.
Where is Jose Andres from?
Jose Andres was born in Mieres, Asturias, Spain on July 13, 1969. He trained at a prestigious culinary school in Barcelona and later worked with legendary chef Ferran Adria at elBulli. He moved to the United States in 1991 and became a naturalized American citizen in 2013.
How many Michelin stars does Jose Andres have?
Jose Andres currently holds 4 Michelin stars across his restaurants: Minibar in Washington D.C. has 2 stars, and Somni in Los Angeles has 2 stars. Both offer intimate tasting menu experiences showcasing his mastery of molecular gastronomy and Spanish-inspired cuisine.
