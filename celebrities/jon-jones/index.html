Training & Diet Approach

The GOAT's Fuel

Jon Jones' approach to nutrition reflects the discipline required at the highest level of MMA:

  • Strict fight camp diet - lean proteins, complex carbs, vegetables
  • Altitude training in Albuquerque requires extra hydration focus
  • Weight management for heavyweight division (moved up from light heavyweight)
  • Post-fight indulgence - steak dinners and celebration meals earned
  • Off-season flexibility while maintaining base fitness

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Jon Jones eat during fight camp?
During fight camp, Jon Jones follows a strict diet focused on lean proteins like chicken and fish, complex carbohydrates such as rice and sweet potatoes, and plenty of green vegetables. His team at Jackson-Wink MMA helps optimize his nutrition for intense training sessions.
Where does Jon Jones train?
Jon Jones trains at Jackson-Wink MMA Academy in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The legendary gym, founded by Greg Jackson and Mike Winkeljohn, has produced multiple UFC champions and is known for its elite coaching and training methods.
Where does Jon Jones eat in Albuquerque?
When not in strict fight camp mode, Jon Jones enjoys Albuquerque's local dining scene including New Mexican cuisine with famous green chile, Mexican restaurants like El Pinto, and steakhouses. The city has a vibrant food scene he can enjoy between camps.
What does Jon Jones eat after a fight?
After fights, especially victories, Jon Jones has been known to celebrate with steak dinners and indulgent meals he avoids during camp. Vegas fight weeks often end at upscale steakhouses and restaurants on the Strip.
How does Jon Jones cut weight?
As a heavyweight, Jon Jones doesn't need to cut as much weight as he did at light heavyweight. His approach focuses on maintaining fighting weight through clean eating and strategic meal timing rather than dramatic weight cuts that lighter fighters endure.
