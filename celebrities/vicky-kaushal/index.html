What is Vicky Kaushal's favorite food?

Vicky Kaushal is a proud Punjabi who loves traditional home-cooked Punjabi food. His favorites include sarson da saag with makki di roti, dal makhani, chole bhature, and parathas. He cherishes his mother's cooking and maintains strong connections to his family's food traditions.

Vicky's Punjabi Food Roots

Proudly Punjabi, Home-Cooked with Love

Unlike star kids, Vicky Kaushal came from a middle-class Punjabi family. His father is stunt director Sham Kaushal. Food traditions are central to his identity:

  • Mother's Kitchen - His ultimate comfort food comes from mom's cooking
  • Sarson da Saag - The quintessential Punjabi winter dish
  • Makki di Roti - Traditional cornmeal bread, eaten with saag
  • Dal Makhani - Rich, buttery lentils the Punjabi way
  • Sunday Family Meals - Big Punjabi family gatherings around food

"Nothing beats maa ke haath ka khana. No restaurant can match it."

Diet for Action Roles

From Comfort Food to Combat Ready

For roles like Uri and Sam Bahadur, Vicky transforms his diet completely:

  • Uri (2019) - Military-style training, high protein, strict discipline
  • Sam Bahadur (2023) - Maintained lean military physique
  • Protein Focus - Chicken, eggs, fish during shoots
  • Carb Control - Reduced Punjabi carbs during training
  • Between Films - Returns to enjoying family food

"When I'm working on action roles, discipline comes first. But when I'm home, I'm eating mom's food."

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Vicky Kaushal's favorite food?
Vicky's favorites are Punjabi home-cooked foods - sarson da saag, makki di roti, dal makhani, chole bhature, and his mother's parathas. He's a proud Punjabi who values family food traditions above restaurant food.
How did Vicky Kaushal prepare for Uri?
For Uri, Vicky followed military-style training with a strict high-protein diet. He reduced carbs, focused on lean proteins, and trained intensely to build the army officer physique. His diet was completely different from his usual Punjabi comfort food.
Is Vicky Kaushal vegetarian?
No, Vicky is not vegetarian. He eats non-vegetarian food, especially chicken and eggs during his training phases. However, many traditional Punjabi dishes he loves (dal makhani, sarson da saag) are vegetarian.
What do Vicky and Katrina eat together?
Vicky and Katrina balance his love for Punjabi food with her clean eating philosophy. She's embraced Punjabi cuisine during family gatherings, while he's learned to appreciate her health-conscious approach. They find balance between their different food backgrounds.
Where does Vicky Kaushal eat in Mumbai?
Vicky primarily enjoys home-cooked meals with family. When dining out, he's spotted at Soho House Mumbai, Bastian (often with Katrina), and occasional Punjabi restaurants. But his mother's kitchen remains his favorite.
