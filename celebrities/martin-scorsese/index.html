Frequently Asked Questions

What are Martin Scorsese's favorite restaurants?
Martin Scorsese frequents classic NYC Italian restaurants like Rao's in East Harlem, where he's a longtime patron. He also loves Patsy's, Il Mulino, and traditional red-sauce Italian-American establishments that remind him of his Little Italy upbringing.
Where did Martin Scorsese grow up?
Martin Scorsese grew up in Little Italy, Manhattan, after his family moved from Queens when he was young. His childhood in Little Italy profoundly shaped both his filmmaking (Mean Streets, Goodfellas) and his lifelong appreciation for Italian-American cuisine.
What kind of food does Martin Scorsese like?
Scorsese loves classic Italian-American cuisine - red sauce dishes, pasta, meatballs, and traditional preparations like Sunday gravy. His Sicilian heritage and Little Italy upbringing established his appreciation for authentic Italian-American cooking.
Can anyone get into Rao's restaurant?
Rao's is notoriously difficult to access - the 10-table East Harlem restaurant operates on a system where regulars "own" tables and pass them down. Scorsese is among the select celebrities and longtime New Yorkers who can secure reservations at this legendary establishment.
