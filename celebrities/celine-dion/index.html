What does Celine Dion eat?

Celine Dion loves French-Canadian cuisine from her Quebec roots. Her favorites include poutine, tourtiere (meat pie), smoked meat sandwiches, and traditional Quebecois dishes. Growing up as the youngest of 14 children in Charlemagne, Quebec, family meals were central to her upbringing and she maintains those food traditions today.

From Charlemagne to Caesars

Celine Dion's remarkable journey from Quebec to Vegas legend:

  • Born in Charlemagne, Quebec - youngest of 14 children
  • Family owned a small piano bar where she first performed
  • Won Eurovision Song Contest for Switzerland in 1988
  • Vegas residency at Caesars Palace (2003-2019) - over 1,100 shows
  • The Colosseum was built specifically for her residency
  • Maintained strong ties to Montreal throughout her career

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Celine Dion's favorite food?
Celine Dion loves French-Canadian cuisine from her Quebec roots. Her favorites include poutine, tourtiere (meat pie), Montreal smoked meat, and traditional Quebecois dishes that remind her of family meals growing up as the youngest of 14 children.
Where does Celine Dion eat in Montreal?
Celine Dion frequents classic Montreal spots including Schwartz's Deli (famous for smoked meat since 1928), Au Pied de Cochon (celebrity chef Martin Picard's restaurant), and upscale French restaurants like Toque! She maintains strong ties to her Quebec hometown.
How long was Celine Dion's Vegas residency?
Celine Dion's Las Vegas residency at The Colosseum at Caesars Palace spanned 16 years (2003-2019), making it one of the most successful residencies in Vegas history. She performed over 1,100 shows, and the venue was built specifically for her.
What is poutine?
Poutine is Quebec's signature dish consisting of French fries topped with cheese curds and brown gravy. It originated in Quebec in the 1950s and has become an iconic Canadian comfort food. Celine Dion, like many Quebecois, considers it a taste of home.
