Frequently Asked Questions

What does Alex Pereira eat?
Alex Pereira follows a Brazilian-influenced diet with rice and beans as staples, quality grilled meats (churrasco), and fresh vegetables. During fight camp, he maintains strict nutrition while enjoying traditional Brazilian foods when not preparing for fights.
Where is Alex Pereira from?
Alex Pereira is from Sao Paulo, Brazil. He grew up in humble circumstances before becoming a kickboxing world champion and later UFC champion in two weight classes. His Brazilian roots strongly influence his food preferences and cultural identity.
What does Poatan mean?
Poatan means "Stone Hand" in Tupi, an indigenous Brazilian language. The nickname references Alex Pereira's devastating knockout power that has earned him championships in kickboxing and two UFC weight classes (middleweight and light heavyweight).
How does Alex Pereira make weight?
Alex Pereira competes at light heavyweight (205 lbs) after moving up from middleweight. His nutrition team helps him maintain fighting weight through clean Brazilian diet principles, controlled portions during camp, and strategic timing of meals and hydration.
Does Alex Pereira eat acai?
As a Brazilian fighter, acai is a natural part of Alex Pereira's diet. The Amazonian superfruit is popular among Brazilian athletes for its energy-boosting properties and antioxidants. It's a staple in Brazilian fight camps and daily nutrition.
