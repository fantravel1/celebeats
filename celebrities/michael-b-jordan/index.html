What does Michael B. Jordan eat?

Michael B. Jordan follows a high-protein, clean eating diet especially during training. His meals include lean proteins (chicken, fish, steak), complex carbs (rice, sweet potatoes), and lots of vegetables. For Creed, he consumed 4,000-5,000 calories daily during bulking phases.

Training Diet

Creed Transformation Diet

To play Adonis Creed, Michael B. Jordan worked with trainer Corey Calliet and followed a strict nutrition plan:

  • Meal 1: Egg whites, oatmeal, fruit
  • Meal 2: Chicken breast, brown rice, vegetables
  • Meal 3: Fish (salmon or tilapia), sweet potato, greens
  • Meal 4: Lean steak, rice, asparagus
  • Meal 5: Protein shake, almond butter
  • Meal 6: Casein protein before bed

Nutrition Principles

  • High protein - 1g per pound of bodyweight
  • Complex carbs - Fuel for intense workouts
  • Meal timing - Every 2-3 hours
  • Hydration - Gallon of water daily
  • Clean eating - Minimal processed foods

Fitness Lifestyle

The MBJ Approach

Michael B. Jordan's approach to fitness and nutrition is about consistency and discipline. Key elements include:

  • Meal prep - Planning meals in advance
  • Personal chef - During intense training periods
  • Flexibility - Allows cheat meals strategically
  • Supplements - Protein powder, BCAAs, vitamins

Working with trainer Corey Calliet, he transforms his physique for roles while maintaining a sustainable approach to nutrition between projects.

Newark Roots

Though born in Santa Ana, California, Michael B. Jordan was raised in Newark, New Jersey. His East Coast upbringing exposed him to:

  • Soul food - Family recipes and Newark spots
  • Italian-American cuisine - Jersey's legendary delis and pizza
  • Portuguese food - Newark's Ironbound district

He's mentioned missing East Coast food, particularly the pizza and deli sandwiches that are hard to replicate in LA.
