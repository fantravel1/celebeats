What is the famous ram-don dish from Parasite?

Ram-don (jjapaguri in Korean) is a combination of two popular Korean instant noodle brands: Chapagetti and Neoguri. In Parasite, the wealthy Park family has their housekeeper prepare it with expensive hanwoo beef, creating a powerful class metaphor. The dish became globally famous after the film's Oscar success.

Parasite's Famous Ram-don

Jjapaguri with Hanwoo Beef

The most iconic food scene in Parasite became a cultural phenomenon:

  • Chapagetti - Black bean noodles (jajangmyeon-style)
  • Neoguri - Spicy seafood udon-style noodles
  • Hanwoo beef - Premium Korean beef (the wealthy twist)
  • Class symbolism - Cheap instant food elevated with expensive ingredients

After Parasite won, ram-don recipes went viral worldwide and instant noodle sales spiked globally.

Award-Winning Filmography

Netflix sci-fi about a girl and her genetically modified super pig. Explores the meat industry and corporate food systems.

Monster movie set along Seoul's Han River. Features memorable family eating scenes in their snack shop.

True crime thriller that established Bong's reputation. Korean cinema masterpiece.

Mother (2009)

Psychological thriller about maternal devotion. Features Korean domestic food culture.

Korean Food Culture

Korean Culinary Heritage

As a Seoul-based filmmaker, Bong is deeply connected to Korean food traditions:

  • Hansik - Traditional Korean cuisine with balanced flavors
  • Korean BBQ - Social dining tradition central to Korean culture
  • Pojangmacha - Street food tents for late-night dining
  • Ramyeon culture - Instant noodles as comfort food staple
  • Banchan - Side dishes that accompany every Korean meal

Frequently Asked Questions

What is ram-don from Parasite?
Ram-don (jjapaguri) is a combination of two Korean instant noodle brands: Chapagetti and Neoguri. In Parasite, the wealthy Park family has their housekeeper add expensive hanwoo beef, creating a class metaphor that became globally famous after the film's Oscar wins.
Where does Bong Joon-ho eat in Seoul?
Based in Seoul, Bong is connected to Korea's vibrant food scene from street food to fine dining. Seoul's Michelin-starred restaurants like Jungsik, Mingles, and Gaon represent the high end, while traditional Korean restaurants and pojangmacha represent everyday Korean food culture.
Does Bong Joon-ho use food in his films?
Food is central to Bong's storytelling. Parasite features the iconic ram-don scene, Okja explores the meat industry, Snowpiercer uses food as a class metaphor (protein blocks vs. sushi), and The Host is set in a family snack shop. Food represents class, humanity, and Korean identity.
What did Bong Joon-ho eat after winning the Oscars?
Bong famously celebrated his historic Oscar wins with In-N-Out Burger. Photos of him holding the California fast food chain's burgers went viral, endearing him further to American audiences.
How many Oscars did Parasite win?
Parasite won four Academy Awards: Best Picture (first non-English film to win), Best Director, Best Original Screenplay, and Best International Feature Film. It was a historic sweep that made Bong Joon-ho a global household name.
