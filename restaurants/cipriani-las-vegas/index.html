What is Cipriani Las Vegas?

Cipriani Las Vegas at Wynn brings the legendary Venice institution to the Strip. Founded in 1931 when Giuseppe Cipriani opened Harry's Bar in Venice, the family invented the Bellini cocktail and Beef Carpaccio. Today, Cipriani Las Vegas maintains that century-old legacy of European elegance, impeccable service, and classic Italian cuisine for discerning celebrities and international guests.

The Original Bellini & Beef Carpaccio

Cipriani invented both the Bellini cocktail (Prosecco and white peach puree) and Beef Carpaccio at Harry's Bar Venice. Order these legendary creations where they were born - the definitive versions of dishes that have been copied worldwide.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the history of Cipriani?
Cipriani began in 1931 when Giuseppe Cipriani opened Harry's Bar in Venice, which became a legendary gathering place for artists, writers, and celebrities. The family invented the Bellini cocktail and Carpaccio dish. Today, the family operates restaurants worldwide maintaining their legacy.
Did Cipriani really invent the Bellini?
Yes. Giuseppe Cipriani invented the Bellini at Harry's Bar Venice in 1948, naming it after the Renaissance painter Giovanni Bellini. The original recipe uses fresh white peach puree and Prosecco, and this exact version is still served at all Cipriani locations.
What is the dress code at Cipriani Las Vegas?
Cipriani maintains an elegant dress code befitting its European heritage. Business attire or smart elegant clothing is expected. No athletic wear, shorts, or overly casual attire. The atmosphere is refined and sophisticated.
