How do you get a reservation at Rao's?

You don't. Rao's has only 10 tables, and each is "owned" by regular patrons who have had standing reservations for decades. Tables are essentially inherited or gifted. The only ways to dine at Rao's are: being invited by a table holder, knowing someone with a standing reservation, or winning a seat at a charity auction. There is no traditional reservation system - no phone calls, no apps, no waitlist.

The Legend

Why Rao's is Different

The Table System

Each of the 10 tables at Rao's has a "regular" - someone who has dined there for years, sometimes generations. These regulars own their tables for specific nights of the week. When they can't make it, they might gift their table to a friend. When a regular passes away, their table often goes to a family member or close friend. It's like a private club with a restaurant attached.

The History

Charles Rao opened the restaurant in 1896 as a simple saloon in East Harlem (then called Italian Harlem). It became a neighborhood institution, serving Southern Italian cooking to the local community. The Rao family has maintained the restaurant for over a century, preserving recipes and traditions that have made it legendary.

The Atmosphere

Stepping into Rao's is like entering a time capsule. Red-checkered tablecloths, Christmas lights year-round, photos of celebrities and locals on the walls, and a jukebox that still plays Sinatra. The intimate space seats only 40 people, and the same staff has worked there for decades. It's old New York preserved in amber.

Rao's FAQs

Getting a reservation at Rao's is nearly impossible for the general public. The restaurant has only 10 tables, and each is "owned" by regular patrons who have had standing reservations for decades. Tables are essentially inherited or gifted. The only ways to dine at Rao's are: being invited by a table holder, knowing someone with a standing reservation, or occasionally through charity auctions. There is no traditional reservation system.

Rao's has hosted numerous celebrities and power players including Martin Scorsese, Jay-Z, Beyonce, Bill Clinton, George Clooney, Derek Jeter, and many sports figures. The restaurant's discretion and exclusive nature makes it a favorite for high-profile diners who value privacy.

Rao's is famous for its impossibly exclusive reservations, classic Italian-American cuisine, and celebrity clientele. Signature dishes include the Lemon Chicken (pollo al limone), Meatballs, Seafood Salad, and their legendary marinara sauce (now sold in grocery stores nationwide). The restaurant has only 10 tables and has operated since 1896.

Dinner at Rao's typically costs $100-200 per person, depending on what you order. While not the most expensive restaurant in NYC, the true cost is the social capital required to secure a seat. The food is traditional Italian-American at premium prices, but the experience and exclusivity are priceless.

Yes! Rao's has expanded to Las Vegas (Caesars Palace) and Los Angeles (Hollywood), where you can actually make reservations. While they share the same recipes and quality, they don't have the same historic atmosphere or impossible exclusivity of the original. You can also buy Rao's marinara sauce in most grocery stores to enjoy at home.
