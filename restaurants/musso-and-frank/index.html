Why is Musso & Frank famous in Hollywood?

Musso & Frank Grill is Hollywood's oldest restaurant, opened in 1919. This legendary establishment has served everyone from Charlie Chaplin and Marilyn Monroe to modern stars like Leonardo DiCaprio and Quentin Tarantino. Famous for its unchanged classic atmosphere, red leather booths, and bartenders who have been making legendary martinis for decades.

Once Upon a Time... in Hollywood

Musso & Frank was prominently featured in Quentin Tarantino's 2019 film "Once Upon a Time in Hollywood," starring Leonardo DiCaprio and Brad Pitt. The restaurant's appearance in the film brought renewed attention to this classic establishment, with many fans visiting to sit in the same booths where Hollywood history was made. Tarantino himself is a longtime regular.

Classic Hollywood Writers' Haunt

The restaurant's famous Back Room was the gathering place for literary legends:

F. Scott Fitzgerald wrote parts of his screenplays here. William Faulkner, Raymond Chandler, and Nathanael West were regulars at the legendary "Writers' Round Table." Charles Bukowski drank at the bar. This literary heritage adds to the restaurant's mystique and draws writers, filmmakers, and Hollywood romantics to this day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Musso & Frank famous?
Musso & Frank Grill is Hollywood's oldest restaurant, opened in 1919. It has been the gathering place for Hollywood legends including Charlie Chaplin, Humphrey Bogart, Marilyn Monroe, and writers like F. Scott Fitzgerald, William Faulkner, and Raymond Chandler. The restaurant is famous for its unchanged classic atmosphere, red leather booths, and legendary martinis.
Which celebrities eat at Musso & Frank today?
Modern celebrities who frequent Musso & Frank include Leonardo DiCaprio, Brad Pitt, Quentin Tarantino, Johnny Depp, George Clooney, and Keith Richards. The restaurant was prominently featured in Quentin Tarantino's Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.
What should I order at Musso & Frank?
The must-order items include the legendary Martini (made tableside by red-jacketed bartenders), Flannel Cakes (their famous pancakes), the Sand Dab Meuniere, Prime Rib, and the Chicken Pot Pie. The menu has remained largely unchanged for decades, which is part of its charm.
Do I need a reservation at Musso & Frank?
Reservations are highly recommended, especially for dinner and weekends. You can call (323) 467-7788 to book. Walk-ins may be accommodated at the bar or during slower hours. Request a booth in the Back Room for the classic Hollywood experience.
