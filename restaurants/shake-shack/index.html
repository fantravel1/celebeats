From Hot Dog Cart to Global Empire

Shake Shack's origin story is the stuff of restaurant legend. In 2001, Danny Meyer created a hot dog cart to support the revitalization of Madison Square Park. The lines got so long that in 2004, a permanent kiosk was built. From those humble beginnings, Shake Shack has grown to over 500 locations across the US, UK, Middle East, and Asia - all while maintaining the quality that made it famous.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which celebrities eat at Shake Shack?
Shake Shack has a devoted celebrity following including Taylor Swift, David Beckham, Chrissy Teigen, John Legend, Emma Stone, Ryan Gosling, and many more. The chain's NYC roots and commitment to quality have made it a favorite for A-listers who want great burgers without pretense.
Where is the original Shake Shack?
The original Shake Shack opened in 2004 in Madison Square Park, Manhattan. It began as a hot dog cart in 2001 as part of an art installation to support the park. The permanent kiosk became so popular that Danny Meyer expanded it into a global chain.
What should I order at Shake Shack?
The must-try items include the ShackBurger (their signature burger with ShackSauce), SmokeShack (with bacon and cherry peppers), crinkle-cut fries, and a concrete (frozen custard blended with mix-ins). The chocolate and peanut butter concretes are particularly beloved.
Is Shake Shack better than In-N-Out?
It depends on what you prefer! Shake Shack offers a more upscale fast-casual experience with thicker patties and premium toppings. In-N-Out is more traditional fast food with thinner smashed patties and a secret menu. Many celebrities love both - they're California vs. NYC burger royalty.
