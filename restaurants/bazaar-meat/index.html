What is Bazaar Meat Las Vegas?

Bazaar Meat is Jose Andres' revolutionary steakhouse at Sahara Las Vegas that reimagines the American steakhouse through a molecular gastronomy lens. The James Beard Award-winning chef combines the finest global cuts - from Japanese A5 wagyu to Spanish Rubia Gallega - with theatrical presentations like Cotton Candy Foie Gras and liquid nitrogen tableside preparations. It's where serious meat lovers meet avant-garde innovation.

Cotton Candy Foie Gras & Wagyu Selection

The playful Cotton Candy Foie Gras exemplifies Jose's whimsical approach, while the global wagyu selection showcases his respect for premium ingredients. From Spanish chuleton to Japanese A5, every cut tells a story.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Bazaar Meat different from other steakhouses?
Bazaar Meat combines Jose Andres' molecular gastronomy expertise with premium steakhouse offerings. Expect cotton candy foie gras, liquid nitrogen-prepared cocktails, and global cuts including Japanese wagyu and Spanish Rubia Gallega beef alongside playful presentations.
Who is Jose Andres?
Jose Andres is a James Beard Award-winning Spanish-American chef known for pioneering molecular gastronomy in America. He owns ThinkFoodGroup restaurants including minibar, Jaleo, and The Bazaar. He's also a humanitarian recognized for World Central Kitchen's disaster relief efforts.
What is the cotton candy foie gras?
The Cotton Candy Foie Gras is Jose's signature whimsical dish featuring seared foie gras wrapped in pink cotton candy. It exemplifies his playful approach to fine dining, combining luxury ingredients with unexpected presentations.
