What is 1587 Prime?

1587 Prime is an upscale steakhouse in Kansas City, Missouri, owned by NFL superstars Travis Kelce and Patrick Mahomes. The restaurant's name cleverly combines their iconic jersey numbers: #15 (Mahomes) and #87 (Kelce). Opened in late 2025, this nearly 10,000 square foot establishment features prime cuts, refined American cuisine, and subtle Chiefs-inspired touches throughout its elegant interior.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who owns 1587 Prime?
1587 Prime is owned by Kansas City Chiefs superstars Travis Kelce (tight end, #87) and Patrick Mahomes (quarterback, #15). The restaurant name cleverly combines their jersey numbers: 15 + 87 = 1587. Both are 3-time Super Bowl champions with the Chiefs.
How do I get a reservation at 1587 Prime?
Reservations can be made online or by phone. Due to the celebrity ownership and prime location, tables book up quickly, especially on Chiefs game days and weekends. We recommend booking 2-3 weeks in advance. Private dining rooms are available for special events and larger parties.
What is the dress code at 1587 Prime?
1587 Prime maintains an upscale casual dress code. Collared shirts and dress shoes are recommended for men, while business casual or cocktail attire works well for women. Chiefs jerseys are welcomed but elevated attire is appreciated for the dining experience.
What celebrities have been spotted at 1587 Prime?
As a celebrity-owned establishment, 1587 Prime regularly hosts Kansas City Chiefs players, coaches, and their families. Notable sightings include Taylor Swift (Travis Kelce's fiancee), Brittany Mahomes, head coach Andy Reid, and various NFL players visiting Kansas City.
What should I order at 1587 Prime?
The Dry-Aged Tomahawk is the showstopper and most Instagram-worthy dish. The Prime Ribeye and Filet Mignon are consistently excellent. Don't skip the Truffle Mac & Cheese as a side. For dessert, the New York Cheesecake is a crowd favorite.
Is 1587 Prime busy on Chiefs game days?
Yes, extremely. Located near Arrowhead Stadium, 1587 Prime sees high demand on game days. For home games, expect the restaurant to be fully booked. Reservations should be made well in advance, and post-game visits may have extended wait times.
