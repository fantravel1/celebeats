What makes Teppanyaki Ginza Onodera special for wagyu?

Onodera sources exclusively A5-grade wagyu from Japan's most prestigious producers - Kobe beef, Matsusaka beef, and Omi beef. Master teppanyaki chefs cook each cut tableside with precision, allowing guests to witness the marbling melt into pure umami perfection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Onodera specializes in the finest A5-grade Japanese wagyu cooked tableside by master teppanyaki chefs. They source from legendary producers including Kobe, Matsusaka, and Omi, representing Japan's wagyu holy trinity.

Expect to spend 30,000-60,000 yen ($230-470 USD) per person depending on the course and wagyu selection. Premium Kobe beef courses cost more. Lunch offers better value with courses from 15,000 yen.

A5 is the highest grade in the Japanese beef grading system. The "A" indicates yield grade (amount of usable meat), while "5" is the top quality score based on marbling, color, firmness, and fat quality. Only about 3% of wagyu achieves A5.
