The Building

Built in 1929 as Charlie Chaplin's private offices and studio, this Spanish Colonial Revival building features original beamed ceilings, arched windows, and stunning architecture. The centerpiece is a massive communal table made from a single walnut tree. Previously home to the legendary Campanile restaurant.

Republique FAQs

Republique is a celebrity brunch favorite visited by Natalie Portman, Reese Witherspoon, Kristen Bell, Dax Shepard, Sarah Michelle Gellar, and Mila Kunis. The stunning historic setting and exceptional pastries make it a go-to for LA's elite.

Republique is famous for its world-class bakery program featuring croissants, kouign-amann, and seasonal pastries. The restaurant also excels at brunch dishes, rotisserie chicken, and dinner service in a stunning 1929 building originally built for Charlie Chaplin.

The building was constructed in 1929 and served as Charlie Chaplin's offices and studio space. The gorgeous Spanish Colonial Revival architecture features original beamed ceilings, arched windows, and a striking central communal table. It previously housed the acclaimed Campanile restaurant.

Reservations are recommended for dinner and weekend brunch but not required. The bakery counter operates on a walk-in basis throughout the day. Book 1-2 weeks ahead for weekend brunch; weeknight dinners are easier to secure.
