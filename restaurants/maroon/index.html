What is Maroon Las Vegas?

Maroon is Chef Kwame Onwuachi's groundbreaking restaurant at Fontainebleau Las Vegas, making history as the first Black-owned restaurant on the Las Vegas Strip. The James Beard Award-winning chef celebrates the African diaspora through Afro-Caribbean American cuisine, blending Louisiana Creole, Caribbean, and West African flavors with fine dining sophistication. It's where culinary excellence meets cultural significance.

Egusi-Crusted Lamb & Jollof Rice

Chef Onwuachi's signature dishes honor his Nigerian and Creole heritage. The Egusi-crusted lamb features traditional West African melon seed crust, while the Jollof Rice has sparked debates about the best version in Vegas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Maroon Las Vegas historic?
Maroon is the first Black-owned restaurant on the Las Vegas Strip. Chef Kwame Onwuachi, a James Beard Award winner, brings his unique Afro-Caribbean American cuisine to the Fontainebleau Las Vegas, celebrating the African diaspora through food.
What type of cuisine does Maroon serve?
Maroon serves Afro-Caribbean American cuisine, blending flavors from the African diaspora including Louisiana Creole, Caribbean, and West African influences with modern fine dining techniques. Signature dishes include the Egusi-crusted Lamb and Jollof Rice.
Which celebrities have dined at Maroon?
Maroon attracts celebrities including Jay-Z, Beyonce, Oprah Winfrey, Kevin Hart, LeBron James, and many athletes and entertainers celebrating Black excellence in dining.
