Why is Bills famous for brunch?

Bill Granger essentially invented modern brunch culture when he opened his first Sydney cafe in 1993. His ricotta hotcakes and creamy scrambled eggs have been called "the world's best" by The New York Times. Bills pioneered the aesthetic, sun-lit cafe experience now copied globally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Bills is famous for the ricotta hotcakes with honeycomb butter and "the world's best scrambled eggs." Bill Granger pioneered the modern brunch culture that has been copied by cafes worldwide.

Bills is reasonably priced at around 2,000-4,000 yen ($15-30 USD) per person for brunch. The ricotta hotcakes are around 1,800 yen, making it an accessible celebrity-loved spot.

Bills has several Tokyo locations including Omotesando (Tokyu Plaza), Odaiba, and the beautiful Shichirigahama location near Kamakura with ocean views. The Omotesando location is most popular with tourists.

Reservations are recommended for weekend brunch, especially at the popular Omotesando location. Weekday mornings are usually easier to walk in. The queue can be long on weekends.
