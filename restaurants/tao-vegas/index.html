What is TAO Las Vegas?

TAO Las Vegas at The Venetian is one of America's highest-grossing restaurants and the Strip's most iconic celebrity destination. The dramatic space features a 16-foot Buddha statue floating above an infinity pool of koi fish, multiple dining levels, and a world-famous nightclub upstairs. From Pan-Asian cuisine to celebrity parties, TAO is where Vegas glamour meets Asian mystique.

The Iconic Buddha & Infinity Pool

TAO's 16-foot Buddha statue, imported from Southeast Asia and floating over an infinity pool, creates one of the most photographed restaurant settings in Vegas. The atmosphere alone has drawn celebrities for nearly two decades.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is TAO Las Vegas known for?
TAO Las Vegas is known for its massive 16-foot Buddha statue floating above an infinity pool, extensive Pan-Asian menu, celebrity nightclub upstairs, and consistent A-list sightings. It's one of the highest-grossing restaurants in America.
Is TAO a restaurant or nightclub?
TAO Las Vegas is both. The main floor features a full-service Pan-Asian restaurant, while TAO Nightclub operates upstairs on weekends. Many celebrities dine downstairs before heading up to the club, making it a complete evening destination.
Do I need reservations for TAO?
Reservations are strongly recommended, especially for weekend dining. The restaurant is massive but fills up quickly, particularly for prime dinner hours and when major events are happening in Vegas.
