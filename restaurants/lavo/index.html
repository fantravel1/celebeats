What is LAVO known for?

LAVO combines upscale Italian dining with nightclub energy. Famous for the one-pound meatball, the restaurant transforms into a party scene late-night with DJs, bottle service, and dancing. It's a TAO Group property alongside TAO, Marquee, and Beauty & Essex - the same team that defines celebrity nightlife.

The Experience

What to Expect

Early Dinner (5-8 PM)

Civilized Italian dining. White tablecloths, professional service, excellent food. A proper restaurant experience. Great for business dinners or date nights before the energy shifts.

Late Night (10 PM - 2 AM)

The transformation begins. DJs start spinning, bottle sparklers light up, and the dining room becomes a party. Tables push back, dancing starts, and the energy goes up. This is when celebrities arrive.

Party Brunch (Vegas Only)

Saturday and Sunday 11 AM - 6 PM. The legendary LAVO Party Brunch features DJs, dancers, champagne showers, and day drinking at its finest. It's Vegas, after all. Bottle packages recommended.

LAVO FAQs

LAVO is known for combining upscale Italian dining with nightclub energy. Famous for the one-pound meatball, the restaurant transforms into a party scene late-night with DJs, bottle service, and dancing. It's a TAO Group property alongside TAO, Marquee, and Beauty & Essex.

LAVO attracts celebrities including Drake, Justin Bieber, Kylie Jenner, Travis Scott, DJ Khaled, Kevin Hart, and many others. The NYC and Vegas locations are popular for birthday celebrations, album release parties, and post-event gatherings.

Reservations are strongly recommended for dinner, especially on weekends. For the late-night party scene, bottle service reservations guarantee seating. Walk-ins are possible but expect a wait. Book through OpenTable or call directly.

LAVO's signature dish is the One-Pound Meatball - a massive, Instagram-famous meatball topped with ricotta and served in marinara sauce. Other popular dishes include Truffle Fries, Chicken Parm, and the Cookie Skillet dessert.

LAVO has an upscale dress code. For dinner, smart casual is acceptable. For late-night and party brunch, dress to impress - no athletic wear, sandals, or shorts for men. Collared shirts recommended. Women typically dress club-ready.
