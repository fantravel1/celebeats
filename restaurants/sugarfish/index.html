The Nozawa Philosophy

Sugarfish was founded on the principles of legendary sushi chef Kazunori Nozawa, known for his strict "no spicy tuna, no California roll" approach at his original restaurant. The chain maintains his standards: warm rice (crucial for proper sushi texture), thick-cut premium fish, crispy nori, and absolutely no modifications. This uncompromising quality is why celebrities trust their sushi to Sugarfish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which celebrities eat at Sugarfish?
Sugarfish has a devoted celebrity following. The Kardashian-Jenner family are frequent visitors, with Kim Kardashian calling it her favorite sushi. Other celebrity regulars include Jennifer Aniston, Jessica Alba, Chrissy Teigen, John Legend, and Reese Witherspoon.
What is the 'Trust Me' at Sugarfish?
The 'Trust Me' is Sugarfish's signature omakase-style meal where the chef selects and serves courses. It includes edamame, sashimi, tuna sushi, salmon sushi, yellowtail sushi, toro (fatty tuna) hand roll, and blue crab hand roll. There's also a 'Trust Me Lite' and 'Nozawa Trust Me' (premium version) available.
Why is Sugarfish sushi different?
Sugarfish uses warm rice (not room temperature) following founder Kazunori Nozawa's traditional technique. They also don't allow modifications, believing their method produces perfect sushi. The fish is high-quality and cut thick, and the seaweed is crispy.
Do I need a reservation at Sugarfish?
Sugarfish accepts reservations through Yelp at most locations, but they also accommodate walk-ins. During peak hours, especially at popular locations like Brentwood and Studio City, expect a wait. The wait is often worth it for the quality sushi at accessible prices.
