What makes Den Tokyo so unique?

Den breaks all the rules of serious kaiseki dining. Chef Hasegawa's "Dentucky Fried Chicken" arrives in a KFC-style box, dishes feature his signature smiley face mascot, and the atmosphere is deliberately casual and fun - yet the cooking underneath is impeccable 2-star quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Den is famous for its playful approach to kaiseki. The "Dentucky Fried Chicken" dish, smiley-face presentations, and warm atmosphere make it unlike any other Michelin-starred restaurant. Despite the fun, the cooking is seriously refined.

The omakase menu at Den costs approximately 23,000-30,000 yen ($180-240 USD) per person. This is relatively affordable for a 2 Michelin star experience in Tokyo. Drinks and service are additional.

Den accepts online reservations through their website. Due to extreme popularity after being named #1 in Asia, book at least 2-3 months in advance. They release tables on specific dates, so check their booking calendar regularly.

Yes, Den has been ranked on both World's 50 Best and was named #1 on Asia's 50 Best Restaurants. Chef Hasegawa has also won the Art of Hospitality Award for the welcoming atmosphere he creates.
