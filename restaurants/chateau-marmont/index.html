Why is Chateau Marmont so famous?

Chateau Marmont is Hollywood's most legendary hotel, a gothic castle on Sunset Boulevard that has been the ultimate celebrity hideaway since 1929. Columbia Pictures chief Harry Cohn famously told his starlets, "If you must get in trouble, do it at the Chateau Marmont." The hotel is known for its absolute discretion, no-photography policy, and status as a sanctuary where celebrities can escape the spotlight.

A History of Hollywood Scandal

The Chateau's walls hold nearly a century of Hollywood secrets. James Dean jumped through a window here to audition for Rebel Without a Cause. Jim Morrison lived here and allegedly fell from a drainpipe. John Belushi died in Bungalow 3 in 1982. Led Zeppelin rode motorcycles through the lobby. The hotel has seen romances, breakups, comebacks, and breakdowns - and kept them all confidential. Today, it operates as a members-only club, ensuring even more privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which celebrities stay and dine at Chateau Marmont?
Chateau Marmont has hosted nearly every major celebrity since the 1930s. Historical guests include Greta Garbo, James Dean, Marilyn Monroe, Jim Morrison, and John Belushi. Modern regulars include Lindsay Lohan, Scarlett Johansson, Keanu Reeves, Lady Gaga, the Olsen twins, and virtually every A-list actor and musician in Hollywood.
Can non-guests eat at Chateau Marmont?
The Chateau Marmont is now a members-only club, making casual dining very exclusive. Restaurant and bar access is primarily for members and hotel guests. Non-members may occasionally be accommodated for dinner with a reservation, but this is not guaranteed. Your best bet is booking a room or being invited by a member.
What is the history of Chateau Marmont?
Opened in 1929, Chateau Marmont was modeled after a Loire Valley castle. It became a celebrity haven because of its discretion - Harry Cohn famously told starlets 'If you must get in trouble, do it at the Chateau Marmont.' It has been the site of scandals, romances, and tragedy, including John Belushi's death in 1982.
Why is there a no-photo policy at Chateau Marmont?
The Chateau's no-photography policy is central to its appeal for celebrities. Guests who violate this policy risk being banned. This discretion is why stars have trusted the hotel for nearly 100 years - it's one of the few places in LA where they can truly let their guard down without fear of tabloid exposure.
