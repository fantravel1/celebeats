What makes The Apple Pan special?

The Apple Pan is LA's iconic burger counter since 1947. Famous for its legendary Hickory Burger with secret sauce, Steak Burger, and homemade pies. The restaurant has just 26 seats at a horseshoe counter - no tables, no reservations, cash only. Celebrities and locals alike wait in line behind eating customers and take whatever seat opens up.

The Counter Experience

There's no host, no reservations, and no special treatment - that's the beauty of The Apple Pan. You wait standing behind seated customers, and when someone finishes, you slide into their spot. The paper-hat-wearing staff have the menu memorized and move with practiced efficiency. Many celebrities love this egalitarian approach - it's the great equalizer where everyone waits their turn.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Apple Pan famous for?
The Apple Pan is famous for its legendary Hickory Burger (with a secret hickory sauce), Steak Burger, homemade pies (especially the banana cream and apple), and its iconic U-shaped counter. Open since 1947, the restaurant has never added tables - all customers sit at the 26-seat horseshoe counter.
Which celebrities eat at The Apple Pan?
The Apple Pan's no-frills authenticity has attracted celebrities for decades. Regular visitors include Adam Sandler, Larry David, David Letterman, Dustin Hoffman, and many entertainment industry executives. The cash-only, counter-service format means celebrities wait in line like everyone else.
Does The Apple Pan take reservations?
No, The Apple Pan does not take reservations. The restaurant is cash-only with counter service only. Everyone waits in line behind customers who are eating, and you take a seat when one opens up. This egalitarian approach is part of its charm.
Is The Apple Pan cash only?
Yes, The Apple Pan is strictly cash only. However, there is an ATM inside the restaurant for your convenience. Most meals run about $15-20 per person with a burger, fries, and pie.
