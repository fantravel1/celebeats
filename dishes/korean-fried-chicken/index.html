What makes Korean fried chicken different?

Korean fried chicken is double-fried, creating an impossibly thin, crispy coating that stays crunchy even under sauce. The first fry cooks the chicken, the second fry at higher heat creates the signature shatter-crisp texture. It's often glazed with sweet-spicy gochujang sauce or soy garlic. The result: crispier than American fried chicken, and that crunch lasts.

The Double-Fry Technique

The secret to Korean fried chicken's legendary crunch is the double-fry technique. First fry at lower temperature (about 350°F) cooks the chicken through. After resting, a second fry at higher temperature (375°F) dehydrates the coating and creates that signature thin, shatteringly crispy crust that stays crunchy even under heavy sauce.

Korean Fried Chicken FAQs

Korean fried chicken is double-fried, creating an impossibly thin, crispy coating that stays crunchy even under sauce. The first fry cooks the chicken, the second fry at higher heat creates the signature shatter-crisp texture.

Chimaek is the Korean combination of "chi" (chicken) and "maekju" (beer). It's a beloved Korean cultural tradition of eating fried chicken with beer, often while watching sports or K-dramas.

Top chains include Bonchon (famous for soy garlic), bb.q Chicken (Korea's largest), Pelicana (oldest, since 1982), Kyochon (premium quality), and Mom's Touch (Korean fast food favorite). All have international locations.

Start with the half & half - half yangnyeom (sweet-spicy) and half soy garlic. This lets you experience both signature Korean flavors. Don't forget the pickled radish (chikin-mu) on the side!
