What is tanghulu?

Tanghulu is a traditional Chinese street food consisting of fresh fruit coated in a crystal-clear hard candy shell. Traditionally made with hawthorn berries, modern versions use strawberries, grapes, oranges, and other fruits. The sugar is heated to hard-crack stage (300-310°F), creating that signature satisfying crunch when you bite through to the juicy fruit inside. It went viral on TikTok for its ASMR appeal and beautiful appearance.

Why It Went Viral

Tanghulu perfectly combines everything TikTok loves: ASMR-worthy sounds (that crunch!), beautiful visuals (crystal-clear coating), satisfying textures (hard candy meets juicy fruit), and DIY potential. The contrast of sweet, crunchy candy with fresh, tangy fruit creates an irresistible sensory experience that translates perfectly to short-form video.

The Science of the Crunch

  • Hard-Crack Stage - Sugar heated to 300-310°F
  • Quick Coating - Dip fast before sugar cools
  • Cold Finish - Ice water sets the candy instantly
  • Crystal Clear - No stirring keeps it transparent
  • Best Fresh - Humidity breaks down the coating

Tanghulu FAQs

Tanghulu is a traditional Chinese street food of fresh fruit coated in crystal-clear hard candy. Traditionally made with hawthorn berries, modern versions use strawberries, grapes, and other fruits. The candy shell cracks satisfyingly when bitten.

The ASMR-worthy satisfying crunch sound, beautiful crystal-clear visual, contrast between sweet candy and fresh fruit, and the meditative DIY process made it perfect for TikTok. Videos have accumulated billions of views.

Tanghulu can be found at Chinese street food vendors, Asian night markets, and increasingly at specialty dessert shops in major cities. Many boba tea shops and Asian dessert cafes now offer it. You can also make it at home with just fruit, sugar, and water.

Skewer dry fruit on sticks. Heat sugar and water (2:1 ratio) to hard-crack stage (300-310°F) without stirring. Quickly dip fruit and immediately plunge into ice water. Eat within hours as humidity softens the candy coating.
