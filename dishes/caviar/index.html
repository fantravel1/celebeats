Types of Caviar

Beluga - The Rarest

Large grey pearls with a buttery, creamy taste. The most expensive and rare caviar, from the Beluga sturgeon. Now banned from wild Caspian harvest, only available farm-raised. Celebrities pay $500+ per ounce for this ultimate status symbol.

$500+ per oz

Osetra - The Celebrity Favorite

Medium golden-brown pearls with a nutty, briny flavor. The most popular choice among celebrities for its perfect balance of quality, taste, and relative availability. Found on most luxury restaurant menus.

$200-400 per oz

Kaluga - The Sustainable Choice

Often called "river Beluga," Kaluga offers large pearls similar to Beluga at a more accessible price point. Sustainably farmed in China, it's become the responsible luxury choice for environmentally-conscious celebrities.

$150-300 per oz

The Perfect Service

  • Served on ice in a mother-of-pearl spoon (never metal)
  • Traditional accompaniments: blinis, creme fraiche, chives
  • Modern pairings: Champagne, vodka, sake
  • Temperature: chilled but not frozen
  • Celebrity move: eat directly from the tin with a spoon

Caviar FAQs

Celebrities order caviar at Petrossian (NYC and LA), Nobu (all locations), Caviar Russe in NYC, Le Bernardin, and high-end steakhouses like Mastro's. Many celebrities also order directly from Petrossian and Russ & Daughters for home delivery.

Celebrity caviar orders typically range from $300-1,000+ per ounce for premium varieties. A typical celebrity caviar service with 50g-100g of Osetra or Kaluga runs $400-800 at fine dining restaurants. Beluga, the rarest, can exceed $500 per ounce.

Beluga is the rarest and most expensive, with large grey pearls and a buttery taste. Osetra has medium golden-brown pearls with a nutty flavor. Kaluga is a sustainable Beluga alternative. Sevruga has small grey pearls with a more intense flavor. Celebrities typically order Osetra or Kaluga for the balance of quality and availability.

Caviar should be served chilled on ice and eaten with a mother-of-pearl or bone spoon (never metal, which affects the taste). Traditional accompaniments include blinis, creme fraiche, and chopped egg whites. However, many purists and celebrities prefer eating it straight from a spoon to appreciate the pure flavor.
