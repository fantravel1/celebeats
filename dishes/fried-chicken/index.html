Celebrity Fried Chicken Spots

Crispy, juicy, and irresistible. From Nashville hot to classic Southern, discover where celebrities satisfy their fried chicken cravings.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Fried Chicken?

Celebrities line up for the best fried chicken just like everyone else. Howlin' Ray's in LA has become the Nashville hot chicken mecca, drawing heat-seekers from Drake to Post Malone. Roscoe's House of Chicken and Waffles is an LA institution serving the classic combo to generations of stars. Gus's World Famous brings Memphis-style to multiple cities. For upscale experiences, The NoMad chicken is legendary among food-obsessed celebrities. Fried chicken represents the ultimate comfort food that no diet can resist.

Fried Chicken Styles Celebrities Love

1

Nashville Hot Chicken

The cayenne-spiced, blazing hot style that became a national obsession. Howlin' Ray's brought it to LA and celebrities can't get enough.

2

Classic Southern

Buttermilk-soaked, perfectly seasoned, golden crispy. The style that started it all, perfected by legends like Gus's and Willie Mae's.

3

Chicken and Waffles

The sweet-savory combo that Roscoe's made famous. Crispy chicken with fluffy waffles and maple syrup is pure celebrity brunch fuel.

4

Korean Fried Chicken

Double-fried for extra crispness, often glazed with gochujang or soy garlic. Momofuku and Korean spots brought this style to celebrity attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Fried chicken has devoted celebrity fans across genres. Beyonce celebrates her Houston roots with Southern fried chicken. Drake and Post Malone are Howlin' Ray's regulars. Kevin Hart and Snoop Dogg are Roscoe's legends. Chrissy Teigen documents her fried chicken adventures. Even fitness icons like The Rock feature epic fried chicken cheat meals.

Top celebrity fried chicken spots in LA include Howlin' Ray's in Chinatown and Pasadena for Nashville hot chicken, Roscoe's House of Chicken and Waffles (multiple locations) for the classic combo, Gus's World Famous for Memphis-style, and upscale options at places like Republique. Each offers a different style but equally excellent fried chicken.

Howlin' Ray's became a phenomenon by bringing authentic Nashville hot chicken to LA. The heat levels challenge even brave eaters, the chicken is perfectly crispy and juicy, and the experience feels authentic. Celebrities appreciate that they wait in line like everyone else (or send assistants) for something genuinely excellent.

Roscoe's has been serving chicken and waffles since 1975, making it part of LA cultural history. It's fed generations of musicians, actors, and athletes. The soul food comfort, late-night hours, and iconic status make it essential for any celebrity who wants to experience authentic LA dining culture.

Most celebrity fried chicken spots are surprisingly affordable: Howlin' Ray's combos run $15-20, Roscoe's plates are $12-18, and Gus's is similar. The NoMad chicken is the luxury outlier at $98 for the whole bird stuffed with foie gras. Great fried chicken doesn't require a celebrity budget.
