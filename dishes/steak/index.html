Steakhouse Guide

Celebrity Steakhouse Orders

From Mastro's to Peter Luger, discover where celebrities indulge in prime cuts and what they order at the best steakhouses.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Steak?

Steakhouses remain the ultimate power-dining destination for celebrities. Mastro's in Beverly Hills is where LeBron James and Mark Wahlberg celebrate big wins. CUT by Wolfgang Puck at the Beverly Wilshire serves wagyu to Hollywood's elite. Peter Luger in Brooklyn has been the NYC steak institution since 1887, attracting everyone from Jay-Z to athletes. STK blends nightclub energy with prime steaks for the younger celebrity crowd. Steakhouses represent celebration, success, and unapologetic indulgence.

Celebrity Steak Cuts Explained

1

Prime Ribeye

The most flavorful cut thanks to its marbling. Athletes like LeBron love it for the protein and taste combination. Best when bone-in.

2

Filet Mignon

The tenderest cut, popular with celebrities who prefer texture over fat. Often ordered medium-rare for optimal tenderness.

3

Japanese A5 Wagyu

The ultimate luxury steak experience. Celebrities order it for special occasions - the marbling creates a buttery, melt-in-your-mouth texture.

4

Tomahawk Ribeye

The Instagram steak. The dramatic long bone makes it perfect for table-side presentation and social media moments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Steakhouses attract a high-protein-loving celebrity crowd. Athletes like LeBron James and Kevin Durant frequent Mastro's. The Rock is famous for epic steak cheat meals. Mark Wahlberg, Sylvester Stallone, and Vin Diesel are action star regulars. Musicians like Jay-Z and Drake prefer the scene at places like Peter Luger and STK respectively.

The top celebrity steakhouses in LA include Mastro's Beverly Hills (athlete favorite), CUT by Wolfgang Puck at the Beverly Wilshire, BOA Steakhouse on the Sunset Strip, and Craig's in West Hollywood (paparazzi central). Each offers prime cuts in upscale settings designed for celebrity comfort and privacy.

Celebrity preferences vary: athletes often order ribeye for maximum protein and flavor. Those watching their diet choose filet mignon for lean protein. For special occasions, Japanese A5 wagyu is the ultimate luxury. Tomahawk ribeye is popular for its dramatic presentation. Most celebrities order medium-rare for optimal texture.

At celebrity steakhouses, expect to pay $60-85 for prime cuts, $120+ for porterhouse for two, and $185+ for Japanese wagyu. With sides, appetizers, and wine, a full dinner easily reaches $200-400 per person. Mastro's butter cake alone is $15. The experience includes premium service and atmosphere beyond just the meat.

Mastro's Beverly Hills combines several elements celebrities love: exceptional prime beef, live piano music creating atmosphere, the famous warm butter cake dessert, professional service that respects privacy, and a crowd of fellow celebrities and athletes. It's become the celebration destination for sports victories, movie premieres, and special occasions.
