Where do celebrities get pancakes?

Clinton St. Baking Company in NYC is widely considered the best pancake destination in America, with celebrities waiting in line for the famous blueberry stack. Sadelle's in SoHo draws the fashion crowd. In LA, The Original Pantry serves 24-hour diner pancakes, while Malibu Farm offers beach-side stacks for the health-conscious.

Top Celebrity Pancake Spots

Clinton St. Baking Company - NYC

Lower East Side | $19-24

The pancake pilgrimage destination. The blueberry pancakes with warm maple butter have been called the best in America. Celebrities wait in line like everyone else. The fluffy, buttermilk perfection is worth every minute.

Tier A

Sadelle's - NYC

SoHo | $22-28

The fashion industry breakfast spot with photogenic pancakes. The fluffy stacks come with berry compotes and whipped butter. Models, editors, and celebrities fuel Fashion Week here.

Tier A

The Original Pantry - LA

Downtown LA | $12-16

The 24-hour DTLA institution that never closes. Classic American diner pancakes that celebrities crave after late nights. Cash only and no-frills, but the pancakes are legendary.

Tier B

Malibu Farm - Malibu

Malibu Pier | $18-22

The beachside breakfast spot with health-conscious pancakes. Buttermilk stacks with fresh fruit overlooking the ocean. Perfect for celebrities who want indulgence with ocean views.

Tier B

Du-par's - LA

Farmers Market, LA | $14-18

The old Hollywood breakfast counter since 1938. Classic hotcakes that celebrities from every era have enjoyed. The Farmers Market location maintains the vintage vibes.

Celebrity Pancake FAQs

Celebrity pancake spots include Clinton St. Baking Company in NYC (world-famous blueberry pancakes), The Original Pantry in LA (24-hour diner), Sadelle's in NYC, and Malibu Farm for health-conscious celebrities. Many celebrities also love IHOP for nostalgic comfort.

Clinton St. Baking Company's blueberry pancakes are considered the best in America by many celebrities. The fluffy buttermilk pancakes with warm maple butter have a cult following. Japanese souffle pancakes at places like Flipper's have gained celebrity popularity for their Instagram appeal.

Yes, many celebrities indulge in pancakes as a cheat meal or comfort food. The Rock is famous for his epic cheat day pancake stacks. Jennifer Garner, Reese Witherspoon, and other celebrities have been photographed at pancake spots. It's a universal comfort food that transcends diet restrictions.

Weekend brunch waits at Clinton St. Baking Company can exceed 2 hours. Celebrities wait in line like everyone else - there's no VIP treatment. Pro tip: go on a weekday morning for shorter waits, or try the Tokyo or Dubai locations.

Clinton St. Baking's pancakes are made with buttermilk for incredible fluffiness, topped with wild Maine blueberries, and served with warm maple butter that melts into the stack. The recipe has remained unchanged since opening and uses premium ingredients that justify the wait.
