The ultimate indulgent side dish at celebrity hotspots. Discover where the A-list orders their crispy, truffle-scented fries.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Truffle Fries?

Celebrities flock to upscale restaurants serving elevated truffle fries. Catch LA is a Kardashian-Jenner family favorite with their signature truffle Parmesan fries. The Nice Guy in West Hollywood draws Justin Bieber and Selena Gomez with crispy fries drizzled in truffle oil. Craig's serves their famous truffle fries to Hollywood's elite nightly. STK locations in LA, NYC, and Miami attract athletes and entertainers alike. The dish has become the go-to shared starter at celebrity dinner tables.

Why Celebrities Love Truffle Fries

1

Perfect for Sharing

Truffle fries are the ultimate group appetizer. At celebrity tables often seating 6-10 people, a plate of truffle fries breaks the ice and gets conversation flowing.

2

Comfort Meets Luxury

Even celebrities crave comfort food. Truffle fries elevate a humble dish to match the upscale settings where stars dine, satisfying cravings without sacrificing status.

3

Instagram Worthy

The golden fries glistening with truffle oil and showered in Parmesan make for an irresistible photo op that celebrities love sharing with their millions of followers.

4

Universal Appeal

Unlike divisive dishes, everyone loves fries. They're the safe choice that pleases picky eaters, dietary restrictions aside, making them perfect for diverse celebrity friend groups.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many A-list celebrities are known truffle fry devotees. Kylie Jenner frequently orders them at Catch LA and posts about them on social media. Justin Bieber and Hailey Bieber are regulars at The Nice Guy where truffle fries are a staple. Drake, Post Malone, the Hadid sisters, LeBron James, and Rihanna have all been spotted enjoying truffle fries at various celebrity hotspots.

The top celebrity truffle fry spots include Catch LA (West Hollywood rooftop), The Nice Guy (West Hollywood late-night scene), Craig's (industry power dinners), Delilah (supper club vibes), and STK (multiple locations). In New York, Catch Steak in Meatpacking is the go-to. Each restaurant offers a unique take on the classic dish with premium truffle oil and quality Parmesan.

Truffle fries combine comfort food appeal with luxury ingredients, making them perfect for celebrity dining. They're ideal for sharing at large tables, photograph beautifully for social media, and satisfy cravings while maintaining an upscale image. The dish has become a status symbol at celebrity hotspots, signaling that you know how to enjoy the finer things while staying relatable.

At celebrity hotspots, truffle fries typically range from $18-26. Catch LA's version is $22, The Nice Guy charges $18, Craig's is $19, and Delilah's truffle frites run $24. Catch Steak in NYC tops out at $26 for their black truffle version. The premium pricing reflects quality truffle oil, aged cheese, and the restaurant's exclusive atmosphere.

Celebrity-favorite truffle fries are elevated in every way. Restaurants use quality white or black truffle oil (not synthetic truffle flavoring), aged Parmesan or Pecorino Romano, hand-cut or perfectly crispy shoestring fries, and fresh herbs. Many spots serve them with house-made truffle aioli. The presentation is always Instagram-ready with the fries artfully arranged and cheese generously shaved tableside.
