Where do celebrities eat lobster?

Celebrities favor Prime 112 in Miami for giant Maine lobsters, Catch LA for dramatic lobster towers, and The Polo Lounge in Beverly Hills for classic thermidor. DJ Khaled famously orders the 5-pound lobster at Prime 112, while the Kardashians are regulars at Catch for their seafood towers.

Celebrity Lobster Styles

Whole Maine Lobster

The showstopper choice. Celebrities go for 4-6 pound giants that arrive tableside as a statement piece. Steamed or grilled with drawn butter. DJ Khaled and Rick Ross are famous for their massive lobster orders at Prime 112.

Lobster Tower

The Instagram-famous seafood tower at Catch LA, featuring lobster tails, claws, and other shellfish stacked dramatically. A Kardashian-Jenner family favorite that's become synonymous with LA celebrity dining.

Lobster Thermidor

The classic French preparation with creamy brandy sauce, beloved at old Hollywood institutions like The Polo Lounge and Musso & Frank. A favorite of actors who appreciate the old-school glamour.

Lobster Mac & Cheese

The comfort food upgrade that's taken over celebrity menus. Philippe Chow's version is a favorite, and Catch's truffle lobster mac attracts A-listers looking for indulgence.

Lobster FAQs

Celebrities favor Prime 112 in Miami for giant Maine lobsters, Catch LA for lobster towers, The Polo Lounge in Beverly Hills for classic thermidor, and Red Lobster's private rooms. In New York, The Grill and Le Bernardin are top picks.

Celebrity lobster orders typically range from $75 for a 2-pound lobster to $300+ for a 5-6 pound giant Maine lobster at restaurants like Prime 112 or Catch. Lobster thermidor at high-end spots runs $85-150.

Whole steamed or grilled Maine lobster is the most popular choice, especially the dramatic 4-6 pound specimens at Prime 112. Lobster mac and cheese has become an Instagram favorite, while classic lobster thermidor remains a staple at old Hollywood haunts.

In her 2016 song "Formation," Beyonce sings about going to Red Lobster as a reward. It was a nod to authentic Southern culture and the chain's place in celebratory dining for many Americans. Sales at Red Lobster spiked 33% after the song's release.
