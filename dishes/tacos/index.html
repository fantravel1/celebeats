Celebrity Taco Spots

From street-style carnitas to upscale fish tacos. Discover where the A-list satisfies their taco cravings.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Tacos?

Celebrities are obsessed with LA's legendary taco scene. Guerrilla Tacos in the Arts District is a favorite of Chrissy Teigen and John Legend for chef-driven creations. Trejo's Tacos, owned by Danny Trejo, attracts fellow celebrities with its laid-back vibes. Sonoratown downtown serves Sonoran-style flour tortilla tacos that Drake and Lizzo have endorsed. Los Tacos No.1 in NYC's Chelsea Market draws everyone from models to musicians. The humble taco has become celebrity comfort food essential.

Why Celebrities Love Tacos

1

Ultimate Comfort Food

Tacos are the ultimate comfort food that transcends status. Many celebrities grew up eating tacos and maintain that connection to their roots despite fame.

2

Quick and Casual

Taco spots don't require reservations or dress codes. Celebrities can pop in, grab tacos, and leave without the formality of high-end restaurants.

3

Late-Night Essential

After events, concerts, or late-night shoots, taco spots are often the only quality food available. Celebrities have made post-midnight taco runs legendary.

4

Authentic Experience

LA's taco scene offers authenticity that celebrities can't find at fancy restaurants. Real street food, real flavors, real connections to Mexican culture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many celebrities are passionate about tacos. Eva Longoria owns her own restaurant and frequently shares her love of Tex-Mex. Danny Trejo owns Trejo's Tacos chain. Selena Gomez grew up on Tex-Mex in Texas. Chrissy Teigen and John Legend are Guerrilla Tacos regulars. Drake, Lizzo, Bad Bunny, the Hadid sisters, and countless other celebrities have been spotted at taco spots in LA and NYC.

The top celebrity taco spots in LA include Guerrilla Tacos (Arts District) for chef-driven creations, Trejo's Tacos (multiple locations) for casual vibes, Sonoratown (DTLA) for Sonoran-style flour tortilla tacos, Tacos Tu Madre (Venice) for fusion tacos, and Damian (Arts District) for upscale Mexican. Late-night, celebrities hit Taco Zone or Leo's Tacos.

Celebrity taco orders vary widely. At Guerrilla Tacos, the sweet potato taco and carnitas are popular. At Sonoratown, everyone gets the carne asada on flour tortilla. At Los Tacos No.1, the adobada (al pastor) is the celebrity choice. Fish tacos are popular among health-conscious celebrities, while carnitas and carne asada satisfy those seeking traditional flavors.

Most celebrity taco spots are surprisingly affordable. Street-style tacos at Sonoratown, Los Tacos No.1, and Trejo's run $4-7 per taco. Even Guerrilla Tacos' elevated creations are only $6-8 each. The exception is upscale Mexican restaurants like Damian where taco tasting menus can run $50+. The accessibility of taco prices is part of their appeal to celebrities.

Taco spots offer celebrities an escape from the spotlight. The casual, no-fuss atmosphere allows them to feel normal and enjoy authentic food without paparazzi or VIP treatment. Many celebrities also have genuine connections to Mexican food from their backgrounds or time living in LA. Tacos represent comfort, community, and culture that transcends wealth and fame.
