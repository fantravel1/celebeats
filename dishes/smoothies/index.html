What Smoothies Do Celebrities Order?

Frequently Asked Questions

Hailey Bieber's "Strawberry Glaze" Erewhon smoothie ($18) contains organic strawberries, dates, avocado, maple syrup, coconut milk, vanilla collagen peptides, and a signature "glaze" made with hyaluronic acid from her Rhode skincare brand. It became Erewhon's best-selling celebrity collaboration and frequently sells out at locations. The smoothie is designed to support skin health from the inside out, aligning with Hailey's beauty brand philosophy.

Multiple celebrities have collaborated with Erewhon on signature smoothies. Current and past collabs include Hailey Bieber (Strawberry Glaze), Bella Hadid (Kinsicle), Kourtney Kardashian (Poosh Detox), Kendall Jenner (Peaches & Cream), Sydney Sweeney, Jake Gyllenhaal (Jake's Fault), and more. Each collaboration reflects the celebrity's personal wellness preferences and often benefits a charity or brand partnership.

Erewhon smoothies cost $18-22+ for several reasons: premium organic ingredients sourced from trusted suppliers, superfood additions like collagen peptides, sea moss, and adaptogens, the celebrity collaboration factor with portion of proceeds going to causes, and Erewhon's positioning as a luxury wellness destination. The high prices also reflect the exclusive experience and community that attracts celebrity clientele.

Celebrity smoothies typically feature premium wellness ingredients: collagen peptides for skin and joints, sea moss for minerals, adaptogens like ashwagandha and maca for stress, organic fruits (often berries or tropical), nut butters for healthy fats, plant-based milks (coconut, oat, almond), vanilla or chocolate protein powder, and natural sweeteners like dates or maple syrup. Many include beauty-focused additions like hyaluronic acid.

Whether celebrity smoothies are "worth it" depends on your perspective. Nutritionally, you could make similar smoothies at home for less. However, the Erewhon experience includes convenience, consistent quality, the social experience of ordering at a celebrity hotspot, and supporting causes through collab proceeds. For many, it's about participating in LA's wellness culture and potentially spotting celebrities rather than just the nutrition.
