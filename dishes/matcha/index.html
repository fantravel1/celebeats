Where Do Celebrities Get Matcha?

Celebrities have made matcha the drink of choice for mindful energy. Cha Cha Matcha with its pink and green aesthetic draws the Instagram crowd including Gigi Hadid. Alfred Tea Room serves pastel-perfect matcha lattes to the fashion set. Erewhon Market offers ceremonial grade matcha in their famous tonic bar. For authentic Japanese preparation, Marugame Monzo in Little Tokyo serves traditional matcha. The green tea revolution has made matcha a celebrity wellness staple.

Frequently Asked Questions

Matcha has a devoted celebrity following. Kourtney Kardashian has made it part of her daily wellness routine, featuring it extensively on Poosh. Gigi and Bella Hadid are Cha Cha Matcha regulars. Gwyneth Paltrow includes matcha in Goop wellness guides. Harry Styles has switched from coffee to matcha. Hailey Bieber, Selena Gomez, and Miley Cyrus are all documented matcha enthusiasts.

Top celebrity matcha spots in LA include Cha Cha Matcha for the Instagram aesthetic, Alfred Tea Room in West Hollywood for the fashion crowd, Erewhon Market for ceremonial grade with adaptogenic add-ins, Urth Caffe for organic consistency, and Cafe Gratitude for the plant-based set. Each offers a unique matcha experience with high-quality ingredients.

Celebrities prefer matcha because it provides caffeine with L-theanine, creating calm alertness without the anxiety or crash that coffee can cause. It's also packed with antioxidants (137x more than green tea), supports skin health, boosts metabolism, and has become a wellness status symbol. The aesthetic appeal for social media is an added bonus.

At celebrity hotspots, matcha lattes typically range from $6-12. Cha Cha Matcha and Alfred Tea Room charge around $7-8 for a latte. Erewhon's ceremonial grade matcha with add-ins can reach $12+. The price reflects the quality of matcha used - ceremonial grade from Japan costs significantly more than culinary grade.

Ceremonial grade matcha is the highest quality matcha, made from the youngest tea leaves with stems and veins removed. It has a vibrant green color, smooth taste without bitterness, and is traditionally used in Japanese tea ceremonies. Celebrity-favorite spots use ceremonial grade because it's smoother and more flavorful than culinary grade, which is better suited for baking and cooking.
