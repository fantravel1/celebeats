Top Celebrity Dim Sum Spots

Din Tai Fung - LA, NYC, Seattle

Multiple Cities | $35-50/person

The Taiwanese dynasty of soup dumplings. Each xiao long bao has exactly 18 pleats. The Glendale and Century City locations are celebrity hotspots. Expect waits, but celebrities often get the VIP treatment.

Tim Ho Wan - NYC

New York City | $25-40/person

The world's cheapest Michelin-starred restaurant from Hong Kong. The BBQ pork buns are legendary. Celebrities love the unpretentious vibes and seriously good dim sum at reasonable prices.

Tier A

Sea Harbour - San Gabriel Valley

Los Angeles Area | $40-60/person

The San Gabriel Valley destination for serious dim sum. Hong Kong-style preparations with premium ingredients. Celebrities willing to venture outside LA proper are rewarded with the best dim sum in the region.

Tier A

Nom Wah Tea Parlor - NYC

Chinatown, NYC | $30-45/person

The oldest dim sum parlor in NYC, operating since 1920. Classic preparations in a historic setting. Celebrities seeking authentic Chinatown vibes make this a destination.

Tier B

Yank Sing - San Francisco

San Francisco | $45-65/person

The upscale SF dim sum institution with cart service. Tech celebrities and visiting A-listers love the Rincon Center location. Premium dim sum with professional service.

Celebrity Dim Sum FAQs

Celebrity dim sum spots include Din Tai Fung (LA, NYC, Seattle), Tim Ho Wan in NYC, Nom Wah Tea Parlor in NYC Chinatown, and Sea Harbour in San Gabriel Valley. Many celebrities visit Hong Kong specifically for dim sum at legendary restaurants.

Xiao long bao (soup dumplings) are the most Instagrammed celebrity dim sum item. Har gow (shrimp dumplings) and siu mai are classics. Celebrities also love BBQ pork buns, turnip cakes, and rice noodle rolls. Adventurous celebrities order chicken feet.

Din Tai Fung is the most celebrity-approved dim sum chain. The Taiwan-based restaurant has locations in LA, NYC, and Seattle that regularly draw A-listers. Their xiao long bao are considered the gold standard. Expect celebrity sightings at the Glendale and Century City locations.

Celebrity dim sum spots range from $25-65 per person depending on the restaurant and order. Din Tai Fung runs $35-50, Tim Ho Wan is $25-40, and premium spots like Sea Harbour can exceed $60 per person with drinks and premium items.

Xiao long bao are Shanghai-style soup dumplings with a thin wrapper containing pork filling and hot soup inside. At Din Tai Fung, each dumpling has exactly 18 pleats. Proper technique involves biting a hole, sipping the soup, then eating the dumpling with ginger-vinegar sauce.
