Celebrity Sweet Tooth

From legendary butter cakes to inventive bakery creations, discover where celebrities satisfy their dessert cravings.

Where Do Celebrities Get Dessert?

Even the most disciplined celebrities have a sweet tooth. Mastro's warm butter cake has become a steakhouse legend that celebrities order religiously. Milk Bar invented crack pie and birthday cake truffles that launched a dessert empire. Sprinkles cupcakes revolutionized the cupcake craze with celebrity endorsement. Salt & Straw serves creative ice cream flavors that draw long celebrity lines. Levain Bakery's massive cookies are NYC celebrity essentials. Desserts represent the ultimate indulgence in an otherwise disciplined celebrity lifestyle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Celebrities gravitate toward legendary desserts with cult followings: Mastro's butter cake, Milk Bar's crack pie and birthday cake, Sprinkles cupcakes, Levain cookies, and Salt & Straw ice cream. The trend is toward signature items from iconic bakeries rather than generic sweets. Celebrities appreciate quality and are willing to indulge in the best.

Top celebrity dessert spots in LA include Mastro's Beverly Hills for butter cake, Sprinkles for cupcakes (with their 24-hour ATM), Salt & Straw for artisan ice cream, Sweet Lady Jane for special occasion cakes, and Porto's Bakery for Cuban pastries. Each offers signature items that have achieved legendary status.

Many celebrities openly admit their dessert obsessions. Taylor Swift is known for baking and her love of Milk Bar. Chrissy Teigen documents her dessert adventures. The Rock features legendary dessert cheat meals on social media. Blake Lively loves cupcakes. Gigi Hadid is a Levain cookie devotee. Even health-conscious stars indulge occasionally.

Mastro's butter cake is a warm, dense cake served with vanilla ice cream that has achieved cult status. The cake is rich with butter (hence the name), has a slightly crispy top, and comes warm so it contrasts perfectly with cold ice cream. It's designed to share but rarely is. Many celebrities cite it as their favorite restaurant dessert.

Most celebrities practice balanced eating that includes occasional indulgences. Many follow the 80/20 rule - eating clean 80% of the time with 20% flexibility for treats. Some save desserts for cheat meals (The Rock's legendary cheat meals are famous). Others portion control, sharing desserts. The key is enjoyment without guilt as part of a sustainable lifestyle.
