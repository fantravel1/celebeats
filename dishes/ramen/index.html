Celebrity Ramen Favorites

Rich, creamy tonkotsu to light shio broths. Discover where the A-list slurps their favorite bowls of ramen.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Ramen?

Celebrities have embraced ramen as the ultimate comfort food. Tsujita on Sawtelle is LA's celebrity ramen destination, known for rich tsukemen dipping noodles. Ippudo locations in NYC attract fashion and music industry stars with their tonkotsu. Daikokuya in Little Tokyo is the late-night choice for actors filming downtown. Momofuku Noodle Bar put celebrity chef David Chang on the map. The humble bowl of noodles has become an A-list obsession.

Best Celebrity Ramen Spots

Tsujita LA Artisan Noodle

Sawtelle, LA $$ Tier A - Verified

The tsukemen (dipping noodles) destination that changed LA's ramen scene. Thick, chewy noodles with impossibly rich pork broth draw celebrities to the Sawtelle corridor.

The Order

Tsukemen - thick noodles, rich tonkotsu dipping broth, chashu, egg ($17) or Tonkotsu Ramen ($16)

Celebrity Fans

Chrissy Teigen John Legend Gwyneth Paltrow

Ippudo

NYC / LA / Multiple $$ Tier A - Verified

The legendary Hakata-style tonkotsu chain from Japan. Their NYC East Village location is a fashion week staple and celebrity late-night destination.

The Order

Shiromaru Classic - Hakata tonkotsu, thin noodles, chashu, kikurage, scallion ($19)

Celebrity Fans

Gigi Hadid Kendall Jenner Karlie Kloss

Momofuku Noodle Bar

NYC East Village $$ Tier A - Verified

David Chang's original restaurant that launched an empire. The pork buns and ramen still draw celebrity fans who want the authentic Momofuku experience.

The Order

Momofuku Ramen - pork and chicken broth, pork belly, poached egg, nori ($21)

Celebrity Fans

Chris Hemsworth Aziz Ansari Anthony Bourdain

Daikokuya

Little Tokyo, LA $ Tier B - Reported

The original Little Tokyo ramen institution, open late for post-event crowds. Their rich, garlicky tonkotsu has satisfied celebrities for decades.

The Order

Daikoku Ramen - signature tonkotsu, chashu, green onion, bamboo, soft-boiled egg ($14)

Celebrity Fans

Post Malone Timothee Chalamet Billie Eilish

Silverlake Ramen

The hipster-friendly ramen spot in LA's coolest neighborhood. Creative specials and a laid-back vibe attract the music and creative industry crowd.

Why Celebrities Love Ramen

1

Ultimate Comfort Food

A hot bowl of ramen is the ultimate comfort food after long shoots, late concerts, or cold NYC nights. It warms the soul like nothing else.

2

Late-Night Availability

Many ramen shops stay open late, making them perfect for celebrities with unpredictable schedules. Post-event ramen runs are a Hollywood tradition.

3

Counter Seating Privacy

Traditional ramen counter seating lets celebrities face forward and enjoy their meal without being approached. The slurping norm also discourages conversation.

4

Japan Connections

Many celebrities developed ramen obsessions during trips to Japan. The dish connects them to memorable travel experiences and Japanese culture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many celebrities are passionate about ramen. David Chang built an empire starting with his Momofuku Noodle Bar. Chrissy Teigen and John Legend are Tsujita regulars. Models like Gigi Hadid and Kendall Jenner frequent Ippudo in NYC. Musicians Post Malone, Harry Styles, and Billie Eilish have all shared their love of ramen. Actor Timothee Chalamet has been spotted at Little Tokyo ramen shops.

The top celebrity ramen spots in LA include Tsujita (Sawtelle) for tsukemen dipping noodles, Daikokuya (Little Tokyo) for classic tonkotsu, Silverlake Ramen for the hipster crowd, and Tatsu Ramen (multiple locations) for customizable bowls. The Sawtelle corridor is particularly popular with celebrities for its concentration of quality Japanese restaurants.

Most celebrities gravitate toward tonkotsu (pork bone broth) ramen for its rich, satisfying flavor. At Tsujita, the tsukemen dipping noodles are the celebrity choice. Ippudo's Shiromaru Classic is popular with models. Some health-conscious celebrities prefer lighter shio (salt) or shoyu (soy sauce) based ramens. Adding extra chashu pork and a soft-boiled egg is standard for celebrity orders.

Ramen is generally considered indulgent rather than health food, but celebrities treat it as comfort food rather than everyday eating. A typical bowl has 500-800 calories with significant sodium. Some celebrities customize their bowls with extra vegetables and lighter broths. Many view ramen as their "cheat meal" reward after strict dieting for roles or shoots.

Ramen shops offer celebrities an escape from fancy restaurants. The casual atmosphere, counter seating, and focus on the food rather than the scene lets them relax. Many shops have quick turnover and limited seating that naturally limits interaction time. The late-night hours also appeal to celebrities with unpredictable schedules who want quality food after midnight.
