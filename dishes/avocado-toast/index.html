Celebrity Avocado Toast

The quintessential LA brunch item that became a celebrity obsession. Discover where the A-list gets their avocado fix.

Where Do Celebrities Eat Avocado Toast?

Celebrities flock to LA's health-conscious cafes for the perfect avocado toast. Cafe Gratitude is a favorite of Miley Cyrus and other plant-based celebs. Sqirl in Silver Lake draws Jessica Alba and Natalie Portman with their famous sorrel pesto rice bowl and elevated toasts. Gjusta in Venice is the Hadid family's go-to, while Joan's on Third in West Hollywood serves the entertainment industry's power breakfast crowd. The dish has become synonymous with California's celebrity wellness culture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many A-list celebrities are known avocado toast devotees. Miley Cyrus frequents Cafe Gratitude for their plant-based version. Gwyneth Paltrow has featured recipes on Goop. The Kardashian family regularly shares their breakfast avocado toasts on social media. Gigi Hadid, Harry Styles, Jessica Alba, and Natalie Portman are all frequently spotted at LA's top avocado toast destinations.

The top celebrity avocado toast spots in LA include Cafe Gratitude (Venice, DTLA) for plant-based celebrities, Sqirl in Silver Lake for the creative crowd, Gjusta in Venice for models and fashion people, Joan's on Third in West Hollywood for industry power breakfasts, and Alfred Coffee for Instagram-worthy moments. Each spot offers a unique take on the classic dish.

Avocado toast aligns perfectly with celebrity lifestyle values: it's healthy (good fats, fiber, nutrients), photogenic for social media, customizable for any diet, and signals participation in California's wellness culture. It's become shorthand for a health-conscious, aspirational lifestyle. The dish is also quick and satisfying, fitting busy celebrity schedules.

At celebrity hotspots, avocado toast typically ranges from $14-18. Cafe Gratitude's "I Am Whole" is $16, Sqirl's version is $15, and Gjusta's is $14. Adding extras like poached eggs, smoked salmon, or premium toppings can push prices to $20+. The price reflects quality ingredients, artisan bread, and the restaurant's cachet.

Celebrity-favorite spots elevate avocado toast beyond the basic version. Key differences include house-baked sourdough or specialty bread, perfectly ripe Hass avocados, quality finishing salts like Maldon, creative toppings (urfa pepper, microgreens, house-made cashew cheese), and beautiful presentation. The experience extends beyond the food to include the aesthetic environment and the chance to spot other celebrities.
