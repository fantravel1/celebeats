Chicago FAQs

The most popular celebrity restaurants in Chicago include Alinea (Lincoln Park), Girl & The Goat (West Loop), RPM Italian (River North), Gibson's Steakhouse (Gold Coast), Avec, and Au Cheval. These restaurants are frequented by local celebrities like Oprah Winfrey, Barack Obama, Chance the Rapper, and Kanye West.

River North and the West Loop are the top neighborhoods for celebrity sightings in Chicago. River North offers upscale steakhouses and Italian restaurants like RPM Italian and Bavette's, while the West Loop (Fulton Market) is home to trendy chef-driven spots like Girl & The Goat and Au Cheval.

Oprah Winfrey has been spotted at several Chicago restaurants including RL Restaurant on Michigan Avenue, Alinea, RPM Italian, and Gibson's Steakhouse. She also famously supported local spots during her years hosting The Oprah Winfrey Show from Chicago's Harpo Studios.

Barack and Michelle Obama are known to frequent Spiaggia, RPM Italian, The Pump Room, and Valois Cafeteria in Hyde Park. They have also been spotted at Topolobampo, Rick Bayless's acclaimed Mexican restaurant, and various South Side neighborhood spots.

Yes, most celebrity-frequented restaurants in Chicago require reservations. Alinea requires advance booking often months ahead. Popular spots like Girl & The Goat, RPM Italian, and Bavette's are notoriously difficult to book on weekends. Au Cheval is walk-in only but expect long waits.
