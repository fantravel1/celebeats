Hongdae FAQs

Celebrities in Hongdae frequent trendy cafes, late-night Korean restaurants, and unique concept dining spots. Yoojung Sikdang (BTS's famous trainee restaurant) is nearby in Mapo-gu. Young K-pop idols often visit Instagram-worthy cafes and Korean BBQ spots in the area.

Hongdae is Seoul's youth culture hub, famous for its indie music scene, street performances, unique cafes, and vibrant nightlife. Named after nearby Hongik University, it attracts young creatives, K-pop trainees, and celebrities seeking an artistic atmosphere.

Hongdae is one of Seoul's best nightlife dining destinations. Restaurants and cafes stay open late, with many 24-hour options. After club hours, celebrities and locals alike flock to late-night Korean BBQ, ramyeon joints, and pojangmacha-style eateries.

Yoojung Sikdang is a humble Korean restaurant near Hongdae where BTS members ate during their trainee days. After the group shared stories about the restaurant, it became a viral destination for ARMY fans worldwide, cementing its status as a K-pop pilgrimage site.
