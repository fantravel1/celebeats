Myeongdong FAQs

Celebrities in Myeongdong enjoy street food stalls, department store restaurants, and hidden local gems. Famous street food includes egg bread, cheese corn dogs, and tteokbokki. Myeongdong Kyoja is legendary for kalguksu noodles, visited by Korean celebrities and tourists alike.

Myeongdong's legendary street food includes gyeran-ppang (egg bread), mozzarella corn dogs, tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), hotteok (sweet pancakes), and grilled seafood. The street food stalls operate mainly in the evening, creating a vibrant food market atmosphere.

Myeongdong Kyoja is a legendary restaurant famous for its kalguksu (knife-cut noodle soup) and mandu (dumplings). Operating since 1966, it has become an institution visited by Korean celebrities, politicians, and tourists seeking authentic Seoul flavors.

Street food stalls in Myeongdong typically open in the late afternoon and operate into the evening. The best time is between 5-9 PM when all vendors are active and the atmosphere is most vibrant. Weekend evenings are especially lively but crowded.
