Traditional Korean Dining Experiences

Samgyetang

Ginseng chicken soup served in traditional earthenware. Tosokchon near Gyeongbokgung is legendary for this restorative dish, visited by K-pop idols and Korean celebrities alike.

Signature Dish

Traditional Korean Tea

Hanok tea houses serve traditional teas like yuja-cha (citron tea), daechu-cha (jujube tea), and omija-cha (five-flavor berry tea) in serene courtyard settings.

Cultural Experience

Tteok (Rice Cakes)

Artisan rice cake cafes serve beautifully crafted traditional Korean sweets. Jilsiru and other specialty shops offer these colorful confections that celebrities love to photograph and share.

Traditional Sweets

Sujebi

Hand-torn noodle soup in rich broth. Samcheong-dong Sujebi is famous for this comforting dish, drawing celebrities seeking authentic Korean soul food in a charming setting.

Comfort Food

Samcheong-dong FAQs

Celebrities in Samcheong-dong frequent traditional hanok cafes, Korean tea houses, and charming restaurants along the main street. The area near Gyeongbokgung Palace offers traditional Korean cuisine, artisan desserts, and quiet dining spots preferred by K-drama stars seeking privacy.

Hanok cafes are coffee shops and tea houses located in traditional Korean houses (hanok). These beautifully preserved buildings offer a unique dining experience with traditional architecture, courtyard seating, and often serve traditional Korean tea and desserts alongside modern coffee.

Yes, Samcheong-dong and the nearby Bukchon Hanok Village appear in countless K-dramas. The area's traditional atmosphere makes it a favorite filming location. Restaurants and cafes in the area often display photos of celebrity visits and drama filming scenes.

Must-try experiences include samgyetang at Tosokchon, traditional Korean tea at a hanok cafe, artisan rice cakes (tteok), and sujebi (hand-torn noodle soup). Combine dining with a visit to Gyeongbokgung Palace and a stroll through Bukchon Hanok Village for the complete experience.
