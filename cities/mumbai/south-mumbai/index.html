South Mumbai Areas

Colaba

Home to the iconic Taj Mahal Palace hotel and its world-class restaurants. Tourist hub with Causeway shopping and heritage architecture. Wasabi, Golden Dragon, and Sea Lounge draw celebrity diners.

Landmark

Nariman Point

Mumbai's business district with corporate fine dining at The Oberoi. Zodiac Grill and Ziya host power lunches and business celebrations. Marine Drive views add to the glamour.

Business District

Worli

Sea-facing luxury with modern high-rises and celebrity penthouses. Restaurants like Estella and various rooftop venues offer stunning views and attract the film industry's elite.

Luxury Living

Kala Ghoda / Fort

Art district with Trishna, Khyber, and Britannia & Co. Heritage buildings house legendary restaurants that have served Mumbai's elite for generations.

Top Restaurants in South Mumbai

Wasabi by Morimoto

Taj Mahal Palace | Japanese | $$$$ | 120+ Celebrity Visits

Iron Chef Masaharu Morimoto's stunning Japanese restaurant at the iconic Taj. Mumbai's finest omakase and sushi draws Bollywood royalty and international celebrities.

Zodiac Grill

The Oberoi | Continental | $$$$ | 80+ Celebrity Visits

Classic fine dining at The Oberoi with art deco elegance. Power dinners and celebrity celebrations in one of Mumbai's most prestigious dining rooms.

Trishna

Kala Ghoda | Seafood | $$$ | 100+ Celebrity Visits

Legendary for butter garlic crab - possibly Mumbai's most famous dish. Unassuming exterior hides a celebrity favorite that has served stars for decades.

Khyber

Fort | North Indian | $$$ | 90+ Celebrity Visits

Iconic North Indian restaurant in a heritage building. Known for kebabs, biryanis, and an atmosphere that has attracted celebrities since 1958.

The Table

Colaba | Farm-to-Table | $$$ | 80+ Celebrity Visits

Modern farm-to-table concept that attracted Bollywood's health-conscious stars. Celebrity couples and industry gatherings in a sophisticated setting.

Britannia & Co.

Ballard Estate | Parsi | $$ | Heritage Institution

Legendary Parsi restaurant famous for berry pulao. A Mumbai institution that has served everyone from the Queen of England rumors to Bollywood stars.

South Mumbai FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in South Mumbai include Wasabi by Morimoto at Taj Mahal Palace (Japanese fine dining), Zodiac Grill at The Oberoi, Trishna (legendary seafood), Khyber (North Indian), and The Table in Colaba. Business tycoons and Bollywood elite frequently dine at these heritage venues.

The Taj Mahal Palace hosts several world-class restaurants including Wasabi by Morimoto (Japanese, Masaharu Morimoto), Golden Dragon (Chinese), Souk (Mediterranean), Masala Kraft (Indian), and the Sea Lounge for high tea. These venues have hosted celebrities and dignitaries for over a century.

Trishna in Kala Ghoda is legendary for its butter garlic crab, considered one of Mumbai's most iconic dishes. The unassuming restaurant has been serving celebrity diners for decades. Many Bollywood stars and international visitors make it a must-visit for seafood lovers.

Best fine dining in South Mumbai includes Wasabi by Morimoto (Japanese), Zodiac Grill at The Oberoi (Continental), Ziya at The Oberoi (Modern Indian), The Table (Farm-to-Table), and Indigo (European). These restaurants offer world-class cuisine and regularly host celebrity events.

South Mumbai encompasses several prestigious neighborhoods including Colaba (Taj Mahal Palace area), Nariman Point (business district), Worli (sea-facing luxury), Fort/Kala Ghoda (art district), and Marine Drive (Queen's Necklace). Each area offers distinct dining experiences favored by different celebrity circles.
