Downtown Los Angeles Celebrity Dining

From industrial-chic Arts District hotspots to glamorous rooftop lounges, DTLA has transformed into LA's most dynamic celebrity dining destination.

Where Do Celebrities Eat in Downtown LA?

Celebrities flock to Downtown LA for its innovative dining scene. Bestia in the Arts District is the top choice for stars like Emma Stone and Ryan Gosling, known for its rustic Italian cuisine in a converted warehouse. Perch offers French dining with stunning rooftop views, popular with Jessica Alba and Chrissy Teigen. Bavel serves acclaimed Middle Eastern food to celebrities seeking creative cuisine, while Bottega Louie is the go-to brunch spot for the fashion and entertainment crowd.

About Downtown Los Angeles

The Scene

Industrial-chic warehouses, rooftop lounges, and museum-district elegance define DTLA's eclectic dining landscape.

Best For

Creative dining, rooftop cocktails, art scene crowds, and warehouse-turned-restaurant experiences.

Peak Times

Weeknight dinners (7-9pm) for celebrities avoiding weekend crowds. Weekend brunch is big at Bottega Louie.

Price Range

$$-$$$$ — More accessible than Beverly Hills with equal quality and better atmosphere.

Downtown LA Dining Districts

Arts District

The celebrity hotspot. Bestia, Bavel, and Factory Kitchen anchor this converted warehouse paradise.

Financial District

Rooftop lounges like Perch and upscale spots for the entertainment industry's business crowd.

Grand Avenue

Museum district dining with Otium and The Broad. Cultural elite and art collectors gather here.

Little Tokyo

Authentic Japanese dining from ramen to sushi, attracting celebrities seeking genuine experiences.

South Park

Near LA Live and Staples Center. Athletes and performers dine here on game and show nights.

Historic Core

Speakeasy bars and restored historic buildings house unique dining concepts.

Frequently Asked Questions

The most popular celebrity restaurants in Downtown LA include Bestia in the Arts District (Emma Stone, Ryan Gosling), Perch for rooftop dining (Jessica Alba, Chrissy Teigen), Bavel for Middle Eastern cuisine (Natalie Portman), and Bottega Louie for brunch (Kim Kardashian). The Arts District has the highest concentration of celebrity-favored spots.

The Arts District has become DTLA's celebrity dining epicenter. Bestia leads the pack as the most sought-after reservation in the neighborhood, followed by its sister restaurant Bavel. The Factory Kitchen attracts actors and directors, while Wurstküche draws a more casual creative crowd. The industrial-chic aesthetic and innovative cuisine appeal to LA's entertainment industry.

Perch is widely considered the best rooftop restaurant in Downtown LA, offering French-inspired cuisine with panoramic views of the city skyline from the 15th floor. The fireplace lounge and outdoor terrace create an intimate atmosphere despite its massive popularity. Other notable rooftop options include Spire 73 at the Wilshire Grand (the highest open-air bar in the Western Hemisphere) and The Roof at the Standard.

Bestia releases reservations exactly 30 days in advance at 10am PT via Resy. Tables book within minutes, so set a reminder and be ready to click. For same-day dining, try the bar area which accepts walk-ins, or check Resy throughout the day for cancellations. Weeknight reservations are slightly easier to secure than weekends. Celebrities often use concierge services or have longstanding relationships with the restaurant.

The main dining areas of DTLA (Arts District, Grand Avenue, Financial District) are safe for evening dining. Restaurants have valet parking and well-lit entrances. Celebrity diners typically use car services or valet to minimize street exposure. The South Park area near LA Live is particularly well-patrolled due to the entertainment venues. As with any urban area, stick to main streets and well-populated areas.
