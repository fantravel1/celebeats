Seattle FAQs

The most popular celebrity restaurants in Seattle include Canlis (Queen Anne), The Walrus and the Carpenter (Ballard), Eden Hill (Queen Anne), Altura (Capitol Hill), and Bateau. These restaurants are frequented by tech executives like Jeff Bezos, Bill Gates, Satya Nadella, and visiting celebrities filming in the Pacific Northwest.

Capitol Hill, Downtown, and South Lake Union are the top neighborhoods for celebrity sightings in Seattle. Capitol Hill offers trendy chef-driven restaurants, Downtown has upscale dining near luxury hotels, and South Lake Union attracts Amazon executives and tech industry leaders.

Amazon executives frequently dine at Canlis, The Walrus and the Carpenter, Bateau, and various South Lake Union restaurants near headquarters. The Amazon Spheres area has become a hub for power lunches, while Canlis remains the go-to for special occasions and important business dinners.

Bill Gates has been spotted at Canlis, The Herbfarm, and various Eastside restaurants near Microsoft's Redmond campus. He's known to enjoy both fine dining experiences and casual local spots, with Canlis being a longtime favorite for celebrations and special occasions.

Yes, most celebrity-frequented restaurants in Seattle require reservations. Canlis often books up months in advance and has a smart dress code. The Walrus and the Carpenter accepts limited reservations but is mostly walk-in with waits. Altura and Eden Hill require advance booking, especially for tasting menus.
