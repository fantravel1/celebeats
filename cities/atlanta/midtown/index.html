Midtown Atlanta

Atlanta's arts and culture district. Where performers from the Fox Theatre, artists from the High Museum, and celebrities seeking sophisticated Southern cuisine converge at acclaimed chef-driven restaurants.

Where do celebrities eat in Midtown Atlanta?

Celebrities in Midtown dine at South City Kitchen for upscale Southern cuisine, Empire State South for James Beard-winning fare, Ecco for Mediterranean, and King + Duke for wood-fired American. Performers from the Fox Theatre and artists are regularly spotted pre-show.

Pre-Theater Dining in Midtown

Midtown's proximity to the Fox Theatre, Woodruff Arts Center, and Atlanta Symphony Orchestra makes it the city's premier pre-theater dining destination. Most restaurants offer early reservations (5-6pm) that allow time for a leisurely meal before 8pm curtains. South City Kitchen and Ecco are the most popular choices.

Midtown Dining Tips

  • Pre-Theater: Book 5-6pm reservations for Fox Theatre shows. Restaurants fill up on performance nights.
  • Brunch: Empire State South brunch is legendary but requires reservations weeks ahead.
  • Parking: Use MARTA's Arts Center station or valet. Street parking is challenging during events.
  • Art Basel: During art events at the High Museum, restaurant traffic spikes significantly.
  • Late Night: Ecco's bar stays lively post-theater. Great for wine and small plates after shows.
