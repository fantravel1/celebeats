Celebrity-Owned Atlanta Restaurants

Which Atlanta restaurants are owned by celebrities?

Ludacris owns Chicken + Beer at Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, serving Southern comfort food. 2 Chainz previously owned Escobar Restaurant & Tapas in Buckhead. Rick Ross operates Wingstop franchises throughout the Atlanta area. The city's hip-hop wealth has created a unique celebrity restaurant ecosystem.

Why Atlanta is the Hip-Hop Dining Capital

Atlanta's rise as the epicenter of hip-hop culture has transformed its dining scene. With record labels, Tyler Perry Studios, and major sports franchises calling the city home, ATL attracts entertainment royalty year-round.

From trap music moguls celebrating platinum records at STK to film industry power lunches at Bones, Atlanta's restaurants have become extensions of the city's creative energy.

The city uniquely blends upscale Southern cuisine with celebrity culture, creating dining experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Atlanta FAQs

Celebrities in Atlanta frequent STK Steakhouse in West Midtown, Bones Restaurant in Buckhead, South City Kitchen in Midtown, and Marcel. Hip-hop artists like Ludacris, T.I., Future, and 2 Chainz are regularly spotted at these establishments.

Ludacris owns Chicken + Beer at Hartsfield-Jackson Airport. 2 Chainz previously owned Escobar Restaurant & Tapas. Rick Ross has Wingstop franchises in the area. Many Atlanta hip-hop artists invest in local restaurant ventures.

Bones Restaurant in Buckhead is considered Atlanta's premier celebrity steakhouse, attracting athletes, musicians, and film industry executives for over 40 years. STK Steakhouse and Marcel are also top celebrity destinations.

Tyler Perry, who operates Tyler Perry Studios in Atlanta, has been spotted at upscale establishments in Buckhead and Midtown including Bones, The Optimist, and local Southern cuisine restaurants near his studio complex.

Buckhead is Atlanta's most upscale dining destination with the highest concentration of celebrity sightings. West Midtown's restaurant row (Westside Provisions District) is increasingly popular. Midtown near the arts district also sees regular celebrity traffic.
