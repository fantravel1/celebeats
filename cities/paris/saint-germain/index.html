Karl Lagerfeld's Cafe de Flore Legacy

Karl Lagerfeld's devotion to Cafe de Flore is legendary in fashion history. The Chanel creative director:

  • Dined at Cafe de Flore every single day for decades
  • Had a dedicated table where he sketched and held meetings
  • Often ordered Diet Coke and simple French dishes
  • Made the cafe a required pilgrimage for fashion industry visitors
  • Yves Saint Laurent, Catherine Deneuve, and countless designers followed

Historic Laperouse

Laperouse is one of Paris's most romantic and secretive celebrity restaurants, famous for:

  • Private salons with mirrors scratched by diamonds (courtesans testing gifts)
  • Dating to 1766, serving literary and political elite for centuries
  • Celebrity diners including Victor Hugo, Guy de Maupassant, Emile Zola
  • Modern visits from Hollywood stars seeking romantic privacy
  • Michelin-starred contemporary French cuisine in historic setting

Saint-Germain FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants include Cafe de Flore, Les Deux Magots, Laperouse, Brasserie Lipp, and Le Voltaire. These historic establishments have served fashion designers, Hollywood stars, and literary icons for over a century.

Karl Lagerfeld famously dined at Cafe de Flore in Saint-Germain-des-Pres every single day for decades. His regular table became legendary, and the cafe remains a pilgrimage site for fashion enthusiasts.

Both are legendary Left Bank cafes, but Cafe de Flore became the fashion crowd's favorite (Karl Lagerfeld, Yves Saint Laurent), while Les Deux Magots has stronger literary associations (Hemingway, Sartre). Both attract celebrities and are located across from each other on Boulevard Saint-Germain.

Laperouse, dating to 1766, features private salons with mirrors famously scratched by courtesans testing diamond gifts. It's one of Paris's most romantic fine dining destinations, hosting celebrities seeking privacy and historic ambiance.
