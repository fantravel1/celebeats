Where do celebrities eat in Paris?

Popular celebrity restaurants in Paris include Cafe de Flore and Les Deux Magots in Saint-Germain, L'Avenue and Fouquet's on the Champs-Elysees, Laperouse for romantic dinners, and L'As du Fallafel in Le Marais. These establishments attract A-list celebrities, fashion icons, and international royalty.

Paris Fashion Week Dining

During Paris Fashion Week, the city transforms into a celebrity dining spectacle. The most exclusive reservations include:

  • L'Avenue - The unofficial Fashion Week headquarters with prime people-watching
  • Cafe de Flore - Morning espressos with designers and models
  • Costes - Late-night scene at Hotel Costes
  • Caviar Kaspia - Luxury caviar and champagne for after-show celebrations
  • Le Voltaire - Discreet fine dining for designers seeking privacy

Paris FAQs

Popular celebrity restaurants include Cafe de Flore, Les Deux Magots, L'Avenue, Fouquet's, Laperouse, and L'As du Fallafel. These spots attract fashion icons, Hollywood stars, and international royalty.

Saint-Germain-des-Pres is the top neighborhood for celebrity spotting, home to iconic cafes like Cafe de Flore where Karl Lagerfeld dined daily. The Champs-Elysees area around L'Avenue is also a major hotspot during Fashion Week.

During Paris Fashion Week, celebrities flock to L'Avenue, Cafe de Flore, Costes, Le Voltaire, and Caviar Kaspia. These restaurants become unofficial headquarters for the fashion industry elite.

Karl Lagerfeld was famously devoted to Cafe de Flore in Saint-Germain-des-Pres, where he dined daily for decades. His signature table became legendary in fashion circles.
