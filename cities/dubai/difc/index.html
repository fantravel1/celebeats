DIFC FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in DIFC include Zuma in Gate Village, La Petite Maison (LPM), Cipriani, Roberto's, and Scalini. The Four Seasons DIFC also houses Nusr-Et Steakhouse by Salt Bae, attracting numerous international celebrities and business elite.

Zuma Dubai in DIFC is one of the city's most celebrated restaurants and a major celebrity hotspot. Known for its contemporary Japanese cuisine, izakaya-style dining, and sophisticated atmosphere, it consistently attracts international celebrities, royalty, and Dubai's elite social scene.

Gate Village is the restaurant and art district within DIFC, featuring a pedestrianized area with world-class dining options. It houses celebrity favorites like Zuma, La Petite Maison, and Roberto's, along with galleries and boutiques in a sophisticated urban setting.

Nusr-Et Steakhouse by Salt Bae is located at the Four Seasons Hotel in DIFC. The restaurant is famous for its gold-wrapped steaks and Salt Bae's theatrical tableside service. It regularly hosts celebrities including the Kardashians, DJ Khaled, and international sports stars.
