Las Vegas Strip FAQs

ARIA Resort & Casino has the highest concentration of celebrity-frequented restaurants on the Strip, including Carbone, Catch, Jean-Georges Steakhouse, and Bardot Brasserie. The Wynn/Encore complex is a close second with Delilah, SW Steakhouse, and Sinatra.

Residency performers like Adele (Caesars Palace), Usher (Park MGM), and Bruno Mars (Park MGM) typically dine at restaurants within their host casino or nearby luxury properties. Popular spots include Nobu at Caesars, Carbone at ARIA, Delilah at Wynn, and Prime Steakhouse at Bellagio.

Joel Robuchon at MGM Grand is the most expensive restaurant on the Las Vegas Strip, with tasting menus starting around $500 per person. It is the only AAA Five Diamond and Michelin 3-star restaurant in Las Vegas. Celebrities seeking ultra-fine dining frequently visit.

TAO Asian Bistro at The Venetian consistently has the most celebrity sightings on the Las Vegas Strip. Its connection to TAO Nightclub, trendy atmosphere, and prime location make it a favorite for A-list celebrities, professional athletes, and musicians.

Celebrities on the Strip typically dine late, with prime celebrity spotting hours between 8 PM and 11 PM. Residency performers often eat post-show between 11 PM and 1 AM. Many high-end Strip restaurants accommodate late reservations specifically for this reason.
