Downtown Las Vegas FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Downtown Las Vegas include Oscar's Steakhouse at The Plaza, Andiamo Italian Steakhouse at The D, Hugo's Cellar at Four Queens, Barry's Downtown Prime at Circa, and Esther's Kitchen in the Arts District. These spots offer classic Vegas glamour and chef-driven cuisine.

Downtown Las Vegas offers a different celebrity experience than the Strip. While sightings are less frequent, celebrities who visit Downtown often seek a more authentic, low-key Vegas experience. The Arts District has become increasingly popular with musicians and artists looking for creative inspiration.

Oscar's Steakhouse at The Plaza is widely considered the best restaurant in Downtown Las Vegas. Named after former Mayor Oscar Goodman, it features a classic steakhouse menu, dome views of Fremont Street, and attracts celebrities and VIPs seeking old-school Vegas elegance.

Downtown Las Vegas has hosted celebrities including Guy Fieri (who films there regularly), Post Malone, Travis Barker, Zac Efron, and various musicians performing at Downtown venues. The Arts District attracts artists and creative industry celebrities seeking the neighborhood's bohemian vibe.

The Fremont Street Experience, Fremont East district, and Arts District are generally safe for visitors, especially during peak dining hours. Circa, Golden Nugget, and The Plaza casinos provide well-patrolled, well-lit environments. As with any urban area, remain aware of your surroundings when walking at night.
