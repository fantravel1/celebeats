Nashville FAQs

The most popular celebrity restaurants in Nashville include Hattie B's Hot Chicken, Prince's Hot Chicken, The Catbird Seat, Rolf and Daughters, and Kayne Prime. Country music stars like Taylor Swift, Keith Urban, and Blake Shelton frequent these spots alongside visiting celebrities.

Broadway and The Gulch are the top neighborhoods for celebrity sightings in Nashville. Broadway (Lower Broad) is the heart of the honky-tonk scene with celebrity-owned bars. The Gulch offers upscale dining like Kayne Prime and The 404 Kitchen.

Nashville has numerous celebrity-owned bars on Broadway including Blake Shelton's Ole Red, Jason Aldean's Kitchen + Rooftop Bar, Florida Georgia Line's FGL House, Dierks Bentley's Whiskey Row, and Luke Bryan's 32 Bridge. These are prime spots for celebrity sightings.

Nashville hot chicken is a local specialty - fried chicken coated in a spicy paste made with cayenne pepper. Prince's Hot Chicken invented it in the 1930s, and Hattie B's popularized it nationally. Celebrities from around the world make pilgrimages for this iconic dish.

CMA Fest (June) and CMA Awards week (November) bring the highest celebrity concentrations. NFL Draft, NHL games (Predators), and major concerts also draw stars. During these events, celebrity-owned bars often see owner appearances.
