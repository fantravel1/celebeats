Broadway FAQs

Top celebrity spots on Broadway Nashville include Blake Shelton's Ole Red, Jason Aldean's Kitchen + Rooftop Bar, Luke Bryan's 32 Bridge, Dierks Bentley's Whiskey Row, and Hattie B's Hot Chicken (downtown location). These celebrity-owned establishments often see appearances by their famous owners and visiting stars.

Major celebrity-owned bars on Broadway include Blake Shelton's Ole Red, Jason Aldean's Kitchen + Rooftop Bar, Luke Bryan's 32 Bridge, Dierks Bentley's Whiskey Row, Florida Georgia Line's FGL House, and John Rich's Redneck Riviera. Miranda Lambert's Casa Rosa is also nearby.

Celebrity owners often appear during CMA Fest (June), CMA Awards week (November), and for special events. Blake Shelton has performed surprise sets at Ole Red. Luke Bryan and Jason Aldean occasionally drop in during major Nashville events. Follow social media for announcements.

For celebrity sightings, visit during CMA Fest (June) or CMA Awards week (November). For a less crowded experience, weekday afternoons are best. Broadway comes alive at night with live music. Weekends are packed but offer the most energy. Most bars open at 10am and go until 3am.

Most honky-tonks on Broadway don't have cover charges - they're free to enter. Tip the bands (they work for tips!). Some rooftop bars and special events may have minimums or covers. Celebrity-owned venues occasionally have covers during major events.

Jason Aldean's Kitchen and Luke Bryan's 32 Bridge are known for the best rooftop experiences with downtown views. FGL House also has excellent rooftop space. For a more historic feel, Acme Feed & Seed's rooftop offers a different vibe with riverfront views.
