The Golden Streets

Via Montenapoleone

The world's most prestigious shopping street. Flagship stores for Prada, Gucci, Versace, and Bulgari line the boulevard, with exclusive restaurants and cafes serving the fashion elite.

Iconic

Via della Spiga

Pedestrian-only fashion paradise. Dolce & Gabbana, Bottega Veneta, and emerging designers create an intimate shopping experience, with charming cafes perfect for celebrity watching.

Exclusive

Via Manzoni

Grand boulevard anchoring the district. Home to Armani's flagship store and restaurant, plus Teatro alla Scala, drawing opera celebrities and fashion icons alike.

Prestigious

Via Sant'Andrea

Connecting the fashion rectangle with boutiques and restaurants. Quieter than Montenapoleone but equally prestigious, favored by celebrities seeking discretion.

Quadrilatero FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Milan's Fashion District include Paper Moon (fashion crowd favorite), Armani/Ristorante (designer dining), Cova (historic cafe), and Il Salumaio di Montenapoleone (elegant Italian). During Fashion Week, these establishments host after-show dinners for designers, models, and celebrities.

The Quadrilatero della Moda (Fashion Quadrilateral) is Milan's luxury fashion district, bounded by Via Montenapoleone, Via della Spiga, Via Sant'Andrea, and Via Manzoni. It houses flagship stores for every major fashion house and exclusive restaurants serving the fashion industry elite.

During Milan Fashion Week, designers host dinners at Paper Moon, Armani/Ristorante, and private venues throughout the Quadrilatero. Brands like Prada, Gucci, and Versace organize exclusive after-show dinners at restaurants within walking distance of their Via Montenapoleone boutiques.

Yes, reservations are essential for Quadrilatero restaurants, especially during Fashion Week (February, June, September) and Design Week (April). Restaurants like Paper Moon and Armani/Ristorante book weeks in advance during these periods. Year-round, weekend reservations are recommended.
