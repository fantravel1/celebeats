Where do celebrities eat in Queen West Toronto?

Queen West's trendy arts scene draws creative celebrities to The Drake Hotel restaurant and rooftop, Baro for Latin-inspired cuisine, Pai Northern Thai for authentic Thai, Campagnolo for rustic Italian, and Oddseoul for Korean fusion. The neighborhood attracts musicians, artists, and celebrities seeking Toronto's coolest vibe.

World's Coolest Neighborhood

Vogue named Queen West one of the world's coolest neighborhoods, and here's why:

  • Graffiti Alley - Toronto's famous outdoor street art gallery
  • Indie Boutiques - Fashion designers and vintage shops
  • Art Galleries - Contemporary and emerging artists
  • Music Venues - The Drake, The Horseshoe Tavern, legendary spots
  • Creative Studios - Design agencies and artist collectives

The neighborhood's creative energy makes it a natural celebrity magnet for those seeking authenticity.

The Drake Hotel Scene

The Drake Hotel is Queen West's celebrity epicenter:

  • Drake Cafe - All-day dining with creative menu
  • Sky Yard - Rooftop with city views and celebrity sightings
  • Underground - Live music venue and late-night scene
  • Art Program - Rotating exhibitions by Canadian artists
  • TIFF Parties - Film festival events and celebrity gatherings

Musicians, artists, and creative industry celebrities make The Drake their Queen West home base.

Queen West FAQs

Celebrity favorites include The Drake Hotel restaurant and rooftop, Baro for Latin cuisine, Pai Northern Thai, Campagnolo for Italian, and Oddseoul for Korean fusion. The creative atmosphere attracts musicians and artists.

Vogue named Queen West one of the world's coolest neighborhoods. It's Toronto's arts and design district with street art (Graffiti Alley), indie boutiques, galleries, and creative restaurants.

Queen West runs from University to Bathurst with established boutiques and The Drake. West Queen West extends from Bathurst to Dufferin with more galleries and edgier vibes. Both attract creative celebrities.
