Benu: SoMa's Crown Jewel

Benu, led by Chef Corey Lee (formerly of The French Laundry), is one of only seven 3-Michelin star restaurants in California. Here's why tech billionaires choose it:

  • 3 Michelin Stars - San Francisco's highest culinary honor
  • Asian-Influenced Tasting Menu - Korean, Chinese, and Japanese techniques
  • Signature Dishes - Thousand-year-old quail egg, XO lobster coral
  • Intimate Setting - Only 40 seats, perfect for private celebrations
  • Tech Elite Favorite - Popular for IPO dinners and investor celebrations

The Marlowe Burger Phenomenon

Marlowe's burger has achieved legendary status in tech circles. Here's why it's become a SoMa institution:

  • The Burger - Dry-aged beef, cheddar, secret sauce, brioche bun
  • Power Lunch Spot - VCs and founders negotiate over fries
  • Truffle Fries - Parmesan and truffle oil, perfect for sharing
  • Anna Weinberg's Empire - Part of the Big Night Restaurant Group
  • No Reservations Drama - Walk-ins only adds to the allure

SoMa FAQs

Top tech celebrity restaurants in SoMa include Benu (3 Michelin stars), Marlowe, The Cavalier, Town Hall, and In Situ at SFMOMA. The neighborhood hosts Twitter/X, Salesforce, and Uber HQ, making these spots popular with tech executives.

Benu is a 3-Michelin star restaurant by Chef Corey Lee, offering contemporary Asian cuisine with Korean and Chinese influences. It's a favorite for tech billionaires celebrating deals, hosting investors, and experiencing one of San Francisco's most acclaimed dining experiences.

During his intensive period at Twitter/X HQ, Elon Musk was known for late-night work sessions with food ordered in. The Mid-Market area near Twitter's offices has numerous restaurants that likely catered to the HQ during this period.

Marlowe's burger uses dry-aged beef, cheddar, and a secret sauce on a brioche bun. It's become a tech lunch institution, with VCs and founders regularly meeting there. The truffle parmesan fries are equally legendary.
