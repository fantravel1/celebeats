Top of the Mark: The Power Meeting Spot

Top of the Mark has been San Francisco's premier power meeting destination since 1939. Here's why tech executives choose it:

  • 360-Degree Views - Panoramic vistas of SF Bay, bridges, and downtown
  • Historic Significance - WWII history, where servicemen had farewell toasts
  • Private Event Spaces - Perfect for board meetings and investor dinners
  • Classic Cocktails - Impeccable martinis and champagne service
  • Impression Factor - The ultimate spot to impress clients and partners

Big 4: Railroad Baron Legacy

Big 4 Restaurant is named after the four railroad tycoons who built Nob Hill: Leland Stanford, Collis Huntington, Mark Hopkins, and Charles Crocker. Today's tech titans continue their legacy of deal-making:

  • Club-Like Atmosphere - Dark wood, leather, and old San Francisco elegance
  • Private Dining - Discreet spaces for confidential discussions
  • Classic Menu - Prime steaks, seafood, and traditional American fare
  • Live Piano - Evening entertainment adds sophistication
  • Tech CEO Favorite - Regular spot for Tim Cook when in SF

The Tonga Room Experience

The Tonga Room at the Fairmont is one of the last great tiki bars in America. Tech workers love it for team celebrations:

  • Indoor Swimming Pool - The bar is built around the Fairmont's original pool
  • Simulated Rainstorms - Every 20 minutes, tropical storms roll through
  • Floating Band - Live music on a boat in the pool
  • Legendary Cocktails - Mai Tais and tropical drinks since 1945
  • Tech Team Favorite - Popular for product launches and team outings
