The Surry Hills Dining Scene

Small Bar Culture

Surry Hills pioneered Sydney's small bar revolution. Intimate venues with curated wine lists and inventive cocktails attract celebrities seeking low-key evenings away from flashy nightclubs.

Iconic

Creative Brunch

The original Bills on Crown Street invented modern brunch culture. Bill Granger's ricotta hotcakes became a global phenomenon, and the cafe remains a celebrity pilgrimage destination.

Legendary

Chef-Driven Dining

Surry Hills attracts ambitious chefs. Firedoor's Lennox Hastie and Nomad's team have created destination restaurants where celebrities experience cutting-edge Australian cuisine.

Innovative

Street Art & Culture

The neighborhood's creative energy extends beyond dining. Galleries, boutiques, and street art create an atmosphere that attracts artists, musicians, and creative celebrities.

Surry Hills FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Surry Hills include the original Bills (birthplace of ricotta hotcakes), Nomad (modern Australian wine bar), Firedoor (wood-fire cooking), and Porteno (Argentine grill). Small bars like The Winery and Dead Ringer attract creative celebrities.

Surry Hills is Sydney's creative hub, known for hipster cafes, independent boutiques, street art, and innovative restaurants. The neighborhood pioneered Sydney's small bar scene and remains home to the original Bills restaurant that started the global brunch movement.

Top celebrity bars in Surry Hills include The Winery (wine bar), Dead Ringer (cocktails and share plates), Shady Pines Saloon (western-themed speakeasy), and Love Tilly Devine (intimate wine bar). These small bars attract Sydney's creative celebrities and industry insiders.

Yes, Surry Hills is easily walkable from Sydney CBD, about 10-15 minutes on foot. The neighborhood sits just east of Central Station, making it convenient for both visitors and locals seeking a more creative dining atmosphere than the business district.
