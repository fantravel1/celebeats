Culinary Guide

Essential Delhi Food Experiences

The must-try dishes that bring celebrities to Delhi's tables.

Bukhara's Dal Makhani

The legendary black dal slow-cooked for 24 hours at ITC Maurya. Every US President visiting India has eaten here. Bill Clinton's signed photo adorns the wall alongside countless dignitaries.

Must Try

Mughlai Heritage

Delhi's Mughal past lives in dishes like biryani, kebabs, and nihari. Karim's near Jama Masjid has served these recipes since 1913. Old Delhi's lanes offer legendary food walks.

Heritage

Modern Indian at Indian Accent

Chef Manish Mehrotra's inventive cuisine reimagines Indian flavors. Blue cheese naan and duck khurchan have made this restaurant a global destination on the World's 50 Best list.

World's Best

Street Food Culture

Chandni Chowk's paranthe wali gali, chaat at Natraj, and butter chicken at Moti Mahal (where it was invented). Even celebrities venture into Old Delhi for authentic street food.

Delhi FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Delhi include Indian Accent (ranked among world's best), Bukhara at ITC Maurya (legendary dal makhani), Dum Pukht (slow-cooked Awadhi cuisine), Le Cirque at The Leela, and Olive Bar & Kitchen in Mehrauli. Politicians, diplomats, cricket stars, and celebrities frequent these venues.

Indian Accent by Chef Manish Mehrotra is consistently ranked among the world's best restaurants. Known for its inventive Indian cuisine that reimagines traditional flavors, it's located in The Lodhi hotel. The restaurant has hosted countless celebrities, politicians, and international dignitaries. Signature dishes include blue cheese naan and duck khurchan.

Best Mughlai food in Delhi includes Bukhara at ITC Maurya (iconic dal makhani and kebabs), Dum Pukht (slow-cooked Awadhi), Karim's in Old Delhi (since 1913), and Moti Mahal (birthplace of butter chicken). Old Delhi's Jama Masjid area offers legendary street food versions of Mughlai classics.

Khan Market is Delhi's most prestigious dining neighborhood, frequented by diplomats, politicians, and celebrities. Mehrauli offers garden restaurants like Olive popular for Bollywood parties. Connaught Place has Indian Accent and historic establishments. Hauz Khas Village attracts younger celebrities with its artistic vibe.

Politicians frequently dine at five-star hotel restaurants like Bukhara (ITC Maurya has hosted every US President visiting India), Dum Pukht, and Le Cirque. Khan Market restaurants are popular for diplomatic meetings. Private dining rooms at these venues allow discreet political gatherings away from public scrutiny.
