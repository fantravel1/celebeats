Connaught Place FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Connaught Place include Indian Accent at The Lodhi (world's best ranking), Farzi Cafe (modern Indian), Veda (theatrical Indian), historic Wenger's bakery, and Kwality (since 1932). The area combines heritage dining institutions with innovative modern cuisine.

Indian Accent by Chef Manish Mehrotra has been featured on the World's 50 Best Restaurants list and Asia's 50 Best. It reimagines Indian cuisine with global influences - signature dishes include blue cheese naan and pork ribs with Kashmiri chili. The restaurant attracts culinary tourists and celebrities worldwide.

Historic CP restaurants include Wenger's bakery (since 1926, legendary patties and pastries), Kwality Restaurant (since 1932, North Indian), United Coffee House (old-world charm), and Embassy Restaurant (since 1948). These institutions have served Delhi's elite for generations.

Connaught Place features distinctive circular Georgian colonial architecture with white colonnaded verandas. Built in the 1930s during British rule, its concentric circles (Inner, Middle, Outer) house shops and restaurants under elegant covered walkways. This heritage setting adds character to dining experiences.

Yes, Connaught Place sees regular celebrity visits, especially at Indian Accent and during film promotions at nearby hotels. Politicians, business leaders, and celebrities visiting Delhi often dine here. The central location makes it convenient for public appearances and events.
