What is Cloud Nine Alpine Bistro?

Cloud Nine Alpine Bistro is the world's most famous ski-in restaurant, located on Aspen Highlands at 10,000 feet elevation. Known for champagne-spraying celebrations, dancing on tables, and a celebrity clientele, Cloud Nine serves Austrian cuisine including fondue and schnitzel in a legendary party atmosphere that has become a pilgrimage for the global elite.

What Makes Cloud Nine Legendary

Everything you need to know about the world's most famous ski lunch.

Champagne Celebrations

The signature Cloud Nine experience involves spraying champagne across the restaurant. Magnums of Veuve Clicquot are opened with fanfare as servers lead the party. It's not unusual to spend $1,000+ on champagne at a single lunch.

Signature

Dancing on Tables

As lunch progresses, guests climb onto tables for dancing. Servers encourage the revelry while European party music fills the Austrian-style chalet. It's a scene that would be impossible to replicate anywhere else.

Legendary

Austrian Cuisine

Beyond the party, Cloud Nine serves excellent Austrian alpine cuisine. The cheese fondue, wiener schnitzel, and apple strudel are genuinely delicious - not just props for the celebration.

Authentic

Ski-In Access Only

Cloud Nine is only accessible by skiing or snowboarding. There's no chairlift access for non-skiers, making the experience truly exclusive. You must navigate intermediate terrain to reach it.

How to Experience Cloud Nine

Essential information for your legendary lunch.

Make Reservations Early

During peak season (Christmas through March), reservations should be made weeks in advance. Contact Aspen Snowmass directly or use your hotel concierge. Walk-ups are nearly impossible during busy periods.

Skiing Required

You must be able to ski or snowboard intermediate terrain to reach Cloud Nine. There is no other access. Plan your route from the Highlands base area - staff can provide directions.

Budget for Champagne

While food is reasonably priced, champagne is the main event. Expect to spend $200-$500+ per person if participating in the full celebration. Bottles start around $150.

Pace Yourself

You still have to ski down after lunch. Know your limits and remember you're at 10,000 feet elevation. Many guests opt for easier runs down after the celebration.
