Gonpachi - The Kill Bill Restaurant

The legendary restaurant that inspired Quentin Tarantino's iconic fight scene.

Gonpachi Nishi-Azabu became a global icon after its dramatic two-story interior inspired the "House of Blue Leaves" fight scene in Quentin Tarantino's Kill Bill Vol. 1 (2003). The restaurant's traditional Japanese architecture with wooden beams, paper lanterns, and open kitchen has made it a must-visit destination for film fans and celebrities alike.

Notable Celebrity Guests

  • Quentin Tarantino - Multiple visits before and after Kill Bill filming
  • Tom Cruise - Dined during Tokyo press tours
  • The Kardashian Family - Kim and family documented visits
  • Justin Bieber - Spotted during Japan tours
  • Uma Thurman - The Bride herself visited the inspiration

What to Order

  • Handmade soba noodles - House specialty
  • Robatayaki (charcoal grilled items)
  • Fresh sashimi selection
  • Wagyu beef skewers

Roppongi FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Roppongi include Gonpachi Nishi-Azabu (the Kill Bill restaurant), RyuGin (3 Michelin stars), Seryna for premium wagyu, and numerous restaurants in Roppongi Hills and Tokyo Midtown. The area is Tokyo's hub for international celebrity dining.

Gonpachi Nishi-Azabu in Roppongi inspired the famous fight scene in Quentin Tarantino's Kill Bill Vol. 1. The restaurant's dramatic two-story interior with wooden beams became iconic after the film's release. Tarantino himself has dined there multiple times.

Roppongi is Tokyo's premier nightlife district for dining, with many restaurants open until late. The area offers everything from sophisticated late-night dining at Michelin-starred establishments to casual izakayas. International celebrities often visit after events or performances.

Notable guests at Gonpachi include Quentin Tarantino (who featured it in Kill Bill), Tom Cruise, the Kardashian family, Justin Bieber, and numerous Hollywood actors visiting Tokyo. The restaurant has become a must-visit celebrity destination.

Reservations are recommended for Gonpachi, especially on weekends and during peak hours. The restaurant accepts online reservations and walk-ins when space is available. Unlike many elite Tokyo restaurants, Gonpachi is relatively accessible for international visitors.
