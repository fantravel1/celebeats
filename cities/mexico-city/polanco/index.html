Signature Dishes in Polanco

The iconic dishes that celebrities travel to experience.

Mole Madre at Pujol

Two moles side by side - one fresh, one aged over 2,500 days. This dish symbolizes the bridge between ancient Mexican tradition and modern innovation. Every celebrity who visits orders it.

Iconic Dish

Tasting Menu at Quintonil

Chef Jorge Vallejo's seasonal tasting menu showcases rare Mexican ingredients. Expect dishes featuring hoja santa, chapulines, and indigenous vegetables transformed into fine dining art.

Chef's Signature

Tacos de Cochinita at Dulce Patria

Elevated street food at its finest. Chef Martha Ortiz transforms classic cochinita pibil into an Instagram-worthy dish that captures Polanco's blend of tradition and luxury.

Celebrity Favorite

Omakase at Pujol

The kitchen counter experience at Pujol offers an intimate view of Olvera's team in action. Limited seats make this the most exclusive dining experience in Mexico City.

Polanco FAQs

Top celebrity restaurants in Polanco include Pujol (World's 50 Best, Chef Enrique Olvera), Quintonil (World's 50 Best, Chef Jorge Vallejo), Dulce Patria (contemporary Mexican), and upscale international restaurants along Avenida Presidente Masaryk.

Pujol is famous for Chef Enrique Olvera's innovative modern Mexican cuisine, particularly the Mole Madre dish featuring two moles - one fresh and one aged over 2,500 days. Consistently ranked among the World's 50 Best Restaurants.

Both Pujol and Quintonil require reservations weeks or months in advance. Book through their official websites or concierge services. Lunch reservations are often easier to secure than dinner.

Polanco restaurants generally expect smart casual to business casual attire. Pujol and Quintonil have a relaxed elegance - no strict dress code, but guests typically dress well. Avoid shorts and flip-flops at fine dining establishments.
