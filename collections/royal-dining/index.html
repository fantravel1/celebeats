Where do royals eat?

Royal families around the world favor restaurants that offer exceptional privacy and discretion above all else. They frequently dine at private members clubs where photography is prohibited, or book private dining rooms at established fine dining establishments. Much royal entertaining happens within palace walls at formal state dinners. When dining out publicly, royals tend to choose venues with a proven track record of protecting VIP guests. Documented favorites include London's exclusive 5 Hertford Street, Monaco's legendary Louis XV by Alain Ducasse, and Copenhagen's world-renowned Noma.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do the British royals eat in London?
The British royals favor establishments like The Ivy for classic British dining, Chiltern Firehouse for trendy gatherings, and Scott's in Mayfair for seafood. They are also frequently spotted at private members clubs like 5 Hertford Street and Annabel's, where strict no-photography policies ensure their privacy. When in Norfolk, Prince William and Catherine frequent local pubs near their Anmer Hall estate.
What restaurants have royal warrants?
Royal warrants are granted to businesses that supply goods or services to the royal household for at least five years. Notable food-related warrant holders include Fortnum & Mason (groceries and hampers), Berry Bros. & Rudd (wine merchants), Champagne Bollinger, Twinings (tea), and various specialty food suppliers. The warrant system recognizes quality and consistent service to the Crown.
Where did Prince Harry propose to Meghan Markle?
Prince Harry proposed to Meghan Markle at Nottingham Cottage, their residence in the grounds of Kensington Palace. The proposal happened while they were attempting to roast a chicken for dinner together. Harry got down on one knee during this intimate, private moment rather than choosing a public restaurant, reflecting the couple's preference for personal, meaningful settings.
What is the Queen's favorite food?
Queen Elizabeth II was known to enjoy simple, traditional British fare. Her documented favorites included chocolate biscuit cake (reportedly requested for her birthday each year), Dundee cake, and classic afternoon tea sandwiches. She preferred her meat well-done and enjoyed game from the royal estates at Balmoral and Sandringham. The late Queen famously avoided garlic and overly spicy foods, a preference that influenced all royal menus.
Do royals eat at public restaurants?
Yes, royals do dine at public restaurants, though they prioritize venues known for discretion and protecting VIP guests. They typically book private rooms or request tables in secluded areas away from main dining rooms. Younger royals like Prince William and Kate are more frequently spotted at restaurants than senior royals, who tend to prefer palace entertaining or ultra-exclusive private members clubs where photography is prohibited.
