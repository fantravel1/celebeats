Why Celebrities Love Private Clubs

San Vicente Bungalows

The most exclusive club in LA with a strict no-phones policy. Founded by Sunset Tower's Jeff Klein, it's become the ultimate A-list sanctuary. Members include George Clooney, Jennifer Aniston, and industry power players.

Tier A

Soho House Culture

Soho House revolutionized the members club for creative industries. Founded for those in film, fashion, media, and the arts. No suits-and-ties pretense. Rooftop pools, Cecconi's restaurants, and screening rooms attract entertainment celebrities.

Tier A

Zero Bond NYC

Opened by nightlife legend Scott Sartiano, Zero Bond quickly became Manhattan's most coveted membership. The multi-floor NoHo space attracts celebrities, fashion figures, and financiers. Members include Travis Scott and Emily Ratajkowski.

Members Club FAQs

Top celebrity clubs include Soho House (40+ locations globally), San Vicente Bungalows in LA, Zero Bond in NYC, The Ned, and Core Club. These offer privacy, quality dining, and networking with entertainment and business elites.

San Vicente Bungalows requires referrals from existing members and has a highly selective committee review. Priority is given to entertainment industry professionals. There's a reported waitlist of thousands. The club enforces a strict no-photo policy.

Soho House "Every House" membership (global access) costs approximately $3,600/year plus a one-time initiation fee. Local memberships are around $2,400/year. Under-27 rates are discounted. Prices vary by location.

Privacy is the main draw - members clubs typically have strict policies against photography and social media. They also offer quality dining, networking with peers, and environments where celebrities can relax without public attention.
