How Celebrities Maintain Privacy

The strategies A-listers use to dine without paparazzi interference:

Members-Only Clubs

San Vicente Bungalows, Zero Bond, and Soho House strictly prohibit photography. Phones are often locked in pouches.

Hidden Entrances

Many restaurants have kitchen doors, parking garage access, or private elevators for celebrity arrivals and departures.

Private Dining Rooms

Restaurants like Carbone, Nobu, and major hotels offer completely private spaces that can be booked exclusively.

Trained Staff

High-end restaurants train staff never to acknowledge celebrities, request photos, or leak information about who is dining.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do celebrities avoid paparazzi at restaurants?
Celebrities use several strategies: dining at members-only clubs like San Vicente Bungalows, using private dining rooms, entering through kitchen or back entrances, booking entire restaurants for private events, and choosing restaurants with strict no-photography policies and trained staff.
What restaurants have private celebrity entrances?
Several high-end restaurants cater to celebrity privacy needs. Giorgio Baldi has a discreet back entrance on PCH. Nobu Malibu offers private areas. Tower Bar at Sunset Tower Hotel has a private elevator. Many NYC restaurants like Carbone have VIP rooms and discreet entrances.
Can regular people join celebrity members clubs?
Most celebrity members clubs require sponsorship by existing members and approval by a membership committee. San Vicente Bungalows and Zero Bond are notoriously difficult to join. Soho House is more accessible but still requires application. Waiting lists can be years long.
