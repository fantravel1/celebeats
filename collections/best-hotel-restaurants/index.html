Legendary Hotel Stories

Chateau Marmont: Hollywood's Castle

Since 1929, Chateau Marmont has been Hollywood's sanctuary. John Belushi died here. Lindsay Lohan lived here. Every major star has a Chateau story. Now members-only, it remains the industry's most exclusive hideaway. The restaurant serves until 1AM for late-night celebrity diners.

Tier A

The Polo Lounge: Where Stars Are Born

The Beverly Hills Hotel's Polo Lounge has hosted Hollywood power since 1941. Marilyn Monroe was discovered here. Every major deal in entertainment history touched these pink tablecloths. The garden patio remains the ultimate power lunch location.

Tier A

Tower Bar: The Industry Clubhouse

Jeff Klein's Sunset Tower Hotel became the industry's living room. The Art Deco Tower Bar hosts Oscar parties, agency dinners, and celebrity regulars like Jennifer Aniston. The McCarthy Salad and Dover Sole are industry standards.

Tier A

The Surf Club: Thomas Keller's Miami

When Thomas Keller opened at Four Seasons Surfside, Miami gained its first true luxury destination restaurant. The historic 1930s building, favored by Winston Churchill and Elizabeth Taylor, now serves Keller's exquisite French-American cuisine.
