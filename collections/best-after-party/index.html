These restaurants offer private dining, late-night service, and the energy A-listers crave post-event.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do celebrities go after the Oscars?
After the Oscars, celebrities head to the official Governors Ball at the Dolby Theatre, followed by the legendary Vanity Fair party. For dinner, top spots include Craig's in West Hollywood, Delilah for supper club vibes, and the iconic Spago Beverly Hills Oscar viewing party hosted by Wolfgang Puck.
What restaurants are popular for celebrity after-parties?
Popular celebrity after-party restaurants include Craig's (Oscar night favorite), Delilah (1920s supper club atmosphere), Catch Steak NYC (post-event VIP room), TAO (Grammy and Met Gala parties), and The Polo Lounge at Beverly Hills Hotel (classic Hollywood power spot).
Which restaurants stay open late for celebrity events?
Several celebrity restaurants accommodate late-night event crowds. Delilah serves until 2am, TAO stays open until 4am on weekends, Catch Steak has flexible hours for events, and many restaurants arrange private buyouts for major awards show after-parties.
How do I spot celebrities at after-party restaurants?
During major events (Oscars, Grammys, Met Gala, Fashion Week), stake out spots like Craig's, Delilah, or TAO between 10pm-1am. Follow paparazzi accounts on social media for real-time updates. Be respectful and never approach celebrities during their meals.
Can regular people dine at after-party restaurants during events?
Most restaurants remain open to the public during awards season, though private events may close portions of the venue. Book far in advance for Oscar or Grammy weekends. Arriving early (7-8pm) gives you the best chance of a table before the after-party crowds arrive.
